# Introducing SPINS: A shared public visualization library of neuroanatomical structures

Brain atlases, or labeled anatomical spatial maps, are essential for understanding brain
structure and function. However, despite the necessity of atlases for interpretation of
results and communication, to date, there is no publicly accessible, precomputed resource
to compare regions across atlases in 3D. We propose an accessible resource to systematically
visualize regions across atlases to reduce the barrier for researchers and clinicians to explore
and communicate anatomy. We built an automated pipeline to render brain regions across
multiple atlases to include gray matter, white matter, and functionally relevant labels. As
a result, we provide a website with page-based navigation across hundreds of regions. Each region
contains a page with an AI-generated overview of the region, along with multiple glass
brain videos and tri-planar visuals.
