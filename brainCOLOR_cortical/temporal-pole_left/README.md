
# temporal-pole

## Overview

The left temporal pole is the most anterior portion of the left temporal lobe, situated rostral to the superior, middle, and inferior temporal gyri and bordering the insula and orbitofrontal cortex medially via the temporal stem. Cytoarchitectonically, it includes agranular and dysgranular cortex and is often associated with Brodmann area 38. This region participates in high-level multimodal integration, including semantic memory, social and emotional processing, and language-related functions such as naming and conceptual knowledge, with strong reciprocal connections to limbic structures (amygdala, hippocampus), orbitofrontal cortex, and other temporal and frontal association areas. Lesions or neurodegeneration affecting the left temporal pole are linked to semantic variant primary progressive aphasia and deficits in word comprehension and object knowledge.
