
# anterior-cingulate-gyrus

## Overview

The right anterior cingulate gyrus is the rostral portion of the cingulate gyrus located on the medial surface of the right cerebral hemisphere, forming part of the limbic cortex and arching above the corpus callosum. It corresponds roughly to Brodmann areas 24, 32, and parts of 33, and is involved in integrating cognitive, emotional, and autonomic information. Functionally, it contributes to error detection, conflict monitoring, decision-making, emotional regulation, pain processing (both physical and social), and modulation of autonomic responses via connections with prefrontal, limbic, and brainstem structures. Cytoarchitecturally, it transitions from agranular to more granular cortex moving dorsally and posteriorly, reflecting its role as an interface between limbic and higher-order associative regions. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “right anterior cingulate gyrus” as a separate entity; a closely related and encompassing structure is described at: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anterior_cingulate_cortex
