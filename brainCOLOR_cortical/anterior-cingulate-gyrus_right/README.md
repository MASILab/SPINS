## Overview
 
The Right anterior-cingulate-gyrus, part of the anterior cingulate cortex (ACC), is located on the medial aspect of the right frontal lobe, arching above the corpus callosum and anterior to the midcingulate and posterior cingulate regions. Cytoarchitectonically, it comprises portions of Brodmann areas 24, 32, and sometimes 33, characterized by dysgranular to agranular cortex that serves as an interface between limbic and frontal executive networks. Functionally, this region is implicated in performance monitoring, error detection, conflict resolution, motivational drive, and the integration of emotional and cognitive information, often showing strong connectivity with the prefrontal cortex, limbic structures (such as the amygdala), and motor control areas. It also contributes to autonomic regulation, including modulation of heart rate and visceromotor responses, particularly during emotionally salient or demanding tasks.
