
# medial-orbital-gyrus

## Overview

The right medial orbital gyrus is a cortical region on the ventromedial surface of the frontal lobe, forming part of the orbitofrontal cortex (OFC) just above the orbits. It is involved in reward valuation, affective decision-making, and the integration of sensory, autonomic, and emotional information to guide goal-directed behavior. This region receives extensive inputs from limbic structures (including amygdala and hippocampus), sensory association cortices, and subcortical neuromodulatory systems, and sends projections to other prefrontal, limbic, and basal ganglia circuits. Functionally, it contributes to encoding the subjective value of stimuli, updating expectations based on feedback, adjusting behavior after reward or punishment, and modulating emotional responses, with right-sided contributions often emphasized in emotional and social processing.
