
# medial-orbital-gyrus

## Overview

The right medial orbital gyrus is a ventral prefrontal cortical region located on the orbital surface of the frontal lobe, medial to the lateral orbital gyri and adjacent to the gyrus rectus and olfactory sulcus. It forms part of the orbitofrontal cortex, a multimodal association area heavily interconnected with limbic, paralimbic, and sensory regions, including the amygdala, hippocampal formation, hypothalamus, and higher-order sensory cortices. Functionally, this region contributes to value-based decision-making, reward processing, emotional evaluation, and the integration of internal states with external stimuli to guide adaptive behavior and social cognition. Cytoarchitectonically, it is composed of granular prefrontal cortex and belongs to the heteromodal association cortex, receiving dopaminergic, serotonergic, and noradrenergic neuromodulatory inputs that support its role in affective regulation and goal-directed behavior. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “right medial orbital gyrus”; a closely related structure is the orbitofrontal cortex: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orbitofrontal_cortex
