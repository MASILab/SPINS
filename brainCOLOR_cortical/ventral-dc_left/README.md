
# Ventral-DC

## Overview

The Left Ventral-DC region in the brainCOLOR atlas refers to the left ventral portion of the diencephalon, a central forebrain division that includes key subcortical structures involved in sensory relay, autonomic regulation, endocrine control, and integration of motor and limbic information. The ventral diencephalic area encompasses territories associated with the hypothalamus and ventral thalamic nuclei, which participate in homeostatic mechanisms (such as thermoregulation, feeding, and circadian rhythms), modulation of visceral and endocrine responses via connections with the pituitary, and channeling processed sensory and motor information to widespread cortical and subcortical targets.
