
# Ventral-DC

## Overview

The Left Ventral-DC region in the brainCOLOR atlas refers to the left ventral portion of the diencephalon, a deep forebrain division that surrounds the third ventricle and lies between the cerebral hemispheres and the midbrain. The diencephalon contains major relay and integrative nuclei, including the thalamus, hypothalamus, epithalamus, and subthalamus, and participates in sensory relay, motor modulation, autonomic and endocrine regulation, and limbic functions. In atlas labeling schemes such as brainCOLOR, “ventral-DC” typically denotes ventral diencephalic structures located inferior to the thalamus and adjacent to the hypothalamic and subthalamic regions, characterized by dense gray matter nuclei and traversing fiber tracts that connect cortical, brainstem, and basal ganglia circuits.
