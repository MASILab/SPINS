
# cuneus

## Overview

The left cuneus is a wedge-shaped cortical region of the medial occipital lobe, situated between the parieto-occipital sulcus superiorly and the calcarine sulcus inferiorly, and extending posteriorly to the occipital pole. It belongs to the visual association cortex and is primarily involved in basic and higher-order visual processing, including aspects of visual attention, spatial orientation, and integration of visual inputs from the contralateral visual field. Cytoarchitectonically, it is largely associated with areas adjacent to primary visual cortex (V1, Brodmann area 17), including parts of areas 18 and 19, and it receives dense input from the lateral geniculate nucleus via the striate cortex, as well as reciprocal cortico-cortical connections with dorsal and ventral visual stream areas. Functionally, activity in the left cuneus has been associated with visual imagery, reading, and tasks requiring visuospatial analysis, and it participates in networks linking occipital, parietal, and frontal regions for coordinated visual cognition.
