
# Cerebellum-Exterior

## Overview

The right cerebellum-exterior corresponds to the lateral cortical surface of the right cerebellar hemisphere, excluding deep nuclei and internal white matter. This region consists primarily of cerebellar cortex organized into foliated gyri (folia) with a characteristic trilaminar structure: the molecular layer (rich in interneurons and parallel fibers), the Purkinje cell layer (a monolayer of large projection neurons), and the granular layer (densely packed granule cells). Functionally, the lateral portions of the cerebellar hemispheres, including the right exterior surface, are strongly implicated in higher-order motor control (planning, timing, and coordination of precise movements) and in cognitive and affective functions via extensive connections with cerebral association cortices and thalamic nuclei. The right cerebellar hemisphere shows prominent contralateral (left-hemispheric) cerebral connectivity, contributing to lateralized functions such as language and executive processing.
