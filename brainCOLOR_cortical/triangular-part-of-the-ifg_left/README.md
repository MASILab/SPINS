
# triangular-part-of-the-IFG

## Overview

The left triangular part of the inferior frontal gyrus (IFG), often corresponding to Brodmann area 45 and commonly referred to as part of Broca’s region, is a cortical subdivision of the frontal lobe located in the dominant (typically left) hemisphere. It occupies the middle portion of the inferior frontal gyrus, positioned between the opercular and orbital parts, and lies anterior to the precentral gyrus and superior to the lateral sulcus (Sylvian fissure). Cytoarchitectonically, this area is involved in higher-order language processing, including semantic retrieval, syntactic processing, and aspects of speech production and comprehension, and is strongly engaged in tasks involving controlled retrieval of verbal information and selection among competing linguistic representations. Functionally, it is interconnected with other language-related regions such as the superior temporal gyrus and middle temporal gyrus, as well as prefrontal and premotor areas, supporting complex language, working memory, and cognitive control functions. There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for the “left triangular part of the inferior frontal gyrus” as defined in the brainCOLOR atlas; a closely related and commonly referenced structure is the inferior frontal gyrus: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inferior_frontal_gyrus.
