## Overview

The left superior frontal gyrus is a dorsal frontal lobe structure located on the lateral and medial surfaces of the left hemisphere, anterior to the precentral gyrus and extending rostrally toward the frontal pole. It participates in higher-order cognitive control, including aspects of working memory, attention, decision-making, and executive functions, and shows strong connectivity with prefrontal, premotor, cingulate, and parietal association areas. Medial portions contribute to self-referential processing and aspects of motor planning, while more lateral regions are involved in complex cognitive operations and top-down modulation of behavior.
