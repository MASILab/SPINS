
# superior-frontal-gyrus

## Overview

The left superior frontal gyrus is a dorsal frontal lobe structure located anterior to the precentral gyrus and superior to the middle frontal gyrus, forming part of the superior frontal region on the medial and dorsolateral surfaces of the cerebral hemisphere. It extends from the frontal pole posteriorly toward the precentral sulcus and is involved in higher-order executive functions, working memory, attention control, and aspects of motor planning, as well as contributing to self-referential and internally directed cognition via its medial portions that interface with the medial prefrontal cortex. Cytoarchitectonically, it encompasses portions of Brodmann areas such as 6, 8, 9, and 10, housing premotor and prefrontal fields that connect extensively with other frontal, parietal, and limbic regions.
