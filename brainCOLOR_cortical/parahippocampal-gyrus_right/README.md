
# parahippocampal-gyrus

## Overview

The right parahippocampal gyrus is a medial temporal lobe cortical region that lies ventral to the hippocampus and adjacent to the fusiform gyrus, forming part of the limbic system and the parahippocampal formation. It contains key subregions such as the entorhinal cortex and parahippocampal cortex, which receive highly processed multimodal sensory input from association cortices and provide a major cortical gateway to the hippocampal formation via the perforant path. Functionally, the right parahippocampal gyrus is strongly implicated in visuospatial and contextual memory, scene perception, and navigation, contributing to the encoding and retrieval of environmental context and spatial layouts. Vascular supply arises primarily from branches of the posterior cerebral artery, and its white matter connections include projections to and from the hippocampus, retrosplenial cortex, posterior cingulate cortex, and prefrontal regions. In neuroimaging and lesion studies, structural and functional alterations of the right parahippocampal gyrus have been associated with disorders of memory, spatial disorientation, and certain anxiety and mood disorders.
