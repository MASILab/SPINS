
# middle-frontal-gyrus

## Overview

The right middle frontal gyrus is a lateral frontal lobe region situated between the superior and inferior frontal gyri, extending anteriorly from the precentral sulcus toward the frontal pole and overlying portions of the dorsolateral prefrontal cortex. Cytoarchitectonically, it encompasses parts of Brodmann areas 9, 46, and adjacent prefrontal fields, and it forms critical nodes in executive, attentional, and working-memory networks, including the frontoparietal control and dorsal attention systems. Functionally, it is implicated in top-down cognitive control, decision-making, response inhibition, and the manipulation of information in working memory, as well as in the coordination of complex, goal-directed behavior and aspects of social cognition.
