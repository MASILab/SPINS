
# middle-frontal-gyrus

## Overview

The right middle frontal gyrus is a cortical region of the dorsolateral prefrontal cortex located in the frontal lobe, lying between the superior and inferior frontal gyri on the lateral surface of the right hemisphere. It is composed primarily of granular and dysgranular neocortex and participates in higher-order executive functions including working memory, attentional control, decision-making, and aspects of cognitive flexibility. This region is heavily interconnected with other prefrontal areas, parietal association cortex, premotor regions, and subcortical structures such as the basal ganglia and thalamus, forming part of large-scale frontoparietal control networks. Functionally, activity in the right middle frontal gyrus has been associated with response inhibition, regulation of goal-directed behavior, and modulation of emotional and social cognition.  

No direct Wikipedia page exists for the right middle frontal gyrus; a closely related and encompassing structure is the middle frontal gyrus: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_frontal_gyrus
