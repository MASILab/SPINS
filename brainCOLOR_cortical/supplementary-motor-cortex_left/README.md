
# supplementary-motor-cortex

## Overview

The left supplementary motor cortex (SMC), often referred to as the supplementary motor area (SMA) on the left hemisphere, is a medial frontal lobe region located on the dorsal surface of the superior frontal gyrus, anterior to the primary motor cortex and bordering the precentral sulcus. It plays a key role in planning, sequencing, and initiating voluntary movements, particularly those that are internally generated, bimanual, or involve complex motor programs. Neurons in this region are involved in motor imagery, temporal organization of movement, and the preparation of speech-related motor acts, with the left side especially implicated in language-related motor planning and in the control of contralateral (right-sided) limb movements. The left SMC is part of distributed motor networks that include premotor cortex, basal ganglia, and cerebellum, and is commonly activated in functional imaging studies of motor preparation and sequence learning.
