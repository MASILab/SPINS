
# gyrus-rectus

## Overview

The left gyrus rectus (straight gyrus) is a medial orbital frontal lobe structure located on the inferior surface of the frontal lobe, adjacent to the olfactory sulcus and medial to the orbital gyri, extending anteriorly from the frontal pole toward the olfactory tract. It is part of the prefrontal cortex and is implicated in higher-order cognitive and affective functions, with proposed roles in reward processing, social and emotional behavior, and aspects of decision-making. The region receives multimodal inputs from other prefrontal areas and limbic structures and contributes to frontal lobe networks involved in personality, motivation, and behavioral regulation. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, the left gyrus rectus is delineated as a distinct cortical parcel within the orbitofrontal cortex, enabling standardized neuroimaging analyses of structural and functional variability in this ventromedial prefrontal region.
