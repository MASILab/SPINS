
# precentral-gyrus

## Overview

The right precentral gyrus is a cortical region of the frontal lobe located immediately anterior to the central sulcus and represents the primary motor cortex (Brodmann area 4) on the right hemisphere. It contains a somatotopic (motor homunculus) map of the contralateral body, with neurons whose activity is crucial for the initiation and execution of voluntary movements, particularly of the left side of the body. Its cytoarchitecture is characterized by a prominent agranular motor cortex with large pyramidal neurons, including Betz cells in layer V, which give rise to corticospinal and corticobulbar projections. Functionally, this region integrates inputs from premotor and supplementary motor areas, somatosensory cortices, basal ganglia, and cerebellum to modulate force, direction, and timing of movement. The right precentral gyrus also contributes to motor aspects of attention, motor imagery, and lateralized motor functions, and lesions can result in contralateral weakness or paralysis (left hemiparesis) and deficits in fine motor control. There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for the “right precentral gyrus” as a separate entity; a closely related structure with an appropriate description is the general precentral gyrus page: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precentral_gyrus
