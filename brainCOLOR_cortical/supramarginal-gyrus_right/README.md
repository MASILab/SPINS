
# supramarginal-gyrus

## Overview

The right supramarginal gyrus is a cortical region of the inferior parietal lobule located in the posterior parietal lobe, arching around the posterior end of the lateral sulcus (Sylvian fissure) and positioned anterior to the angular gyrus and posterior to the postcentral gyrus. Cytoarchitectonically, it largely corresponds to portions of Brodmann area 40, and structurally it is supplied primarily by branches of the middle cerebral artery. Functionally, the right supramarginal gyrus is implicated in multimodal integration of somatosensory, auditory, and visual information, contributing to spatial attention, proprioception, emotional prosody, aspects of social cognition (including empathy), and phonological processing. It forms part of frontoparietal and temporoparietal networks involved in attentional reorienting, body schema, and language-related processes, and is frequently examined in neuroimaging studies of neglect, dyslexia, and disorders of social cognition.
