
# supramarginal-gyrus

## Overview

The right supramarginal gyrus (R-SMG), as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas, is a cortical region of the inferior parietal lobule located at the posterior end of the lateral fissure, wrapping around the termination of the Sylvian fissure and extending onto the lateral surface of the parietal lobe. It lies anterior to the angular gyrus and posterior to the postcentral gyrus, bordering regions associated with somatosensory processing, language, and multimodal integration. Cytoarchitectonically, it corresponds largely to portions of Brodmann area 40, a heteromodal association area implicated in phonological processing, sensorimotor integration, spatial attention, and aspects of social cognition such as empathy and perspective-taking. The R-SMG participates in networks that integrate auditory, visual, and somatosensory information, contributing to higher-order functions including body schema representation and the detection of emotional and social cues. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “right supramarginal gyrus” as a separate entity; a closely related and encompassing structure is described at: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supramarginal_gyrus
