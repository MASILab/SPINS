
# superior-parietal-lobule

## Overview

The left superior parietal lobule is a cortical region of the superior parietal cortex located on the dorsal surface of the parietal lobe, posterior to the postcentral gyrus and anterior to the parieto-occipital sulcus, forming part of Brodmann areas primarily 5 and 7. It is heavily interconnected with primary and secondary somatosensory cortices, premotor areas, visual association regions in the occipital and parietal lobes, and subcortical structures involved in sensorimotor integration. Functionally, the left superior parietal lobule contributes to multimodal sensory integration, spatial attention, body schema representation, and the guidance of visually directed and goal-directed movements, including hand–eye coordination and reaching. It plays an important role in higher-order somatosensory processing and spatial aspects of working memory, and lesions in this region can result in deficits such as impaired spatial orientation, optic ataxia, and elements of hemispatial neglect or apraxia, often more evident in complex visuomotor tasks.
