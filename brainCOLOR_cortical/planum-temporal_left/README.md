
# planum-temporal

## Overview

The left planum temporale is a triangular region of neocortex on the superior temporal plane, lying posterior to Heschl’s gyrus within the posterior superior temporal lobe and forming part of the Sylvian fissure’s floor. It is classically associated with language-dominant (usually left) hemisphere specialization, implicated in phonological processing, auditory scene analysis, and aspects of speech perception and comprehension. Cytoarchitectonically, it overlaps portions of auditory association cortex, including parts of Brodmann areas 22 and adjacent regions, and exhibits marked interhemispheric asymmetry in many individuals, often larger on the left than the right. Functionally, the left planum temporale participates in transforming complex acoustic inputs into linguistic representations, integrating auditory features and contributing to the mapping of sound to meaning and articulation. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “left planum temporale” as a separate structure; a related entry is the general “Planum temporale”: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planum_temporale
