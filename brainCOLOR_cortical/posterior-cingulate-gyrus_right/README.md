
# posterior-cingulate-gyrus

## Overview

The right posterior cingulate gyrus is a medial cortical region located in the posterior part of the cingulate cortex, arching above the corpus callosum and forming part of the limbic lobe. It is positioned posterior to the midcingulate area and anterior to the precuneus, and is interconnected with medial parietal, medial prefrontal, and medial temporal structures, including the hippocampal formation. Functionally, the posterior cingulate gyrus is a central hub of the default mode network, contributing to internally directed cognition such as autobiographical memory, self-referential processing, and aspects of spatial orientation and navigation. It receives and integrates multimodal inputs and is metabolically active at rest; alterations in its activity and connectivity have been implicated in neurodegenerative disorders (notably Alzheimer’s disease), psychiatric conditions, and disorders of consciousness.
