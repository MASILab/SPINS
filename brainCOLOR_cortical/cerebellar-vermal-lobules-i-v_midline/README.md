
# Cerebellar-Vermal-Lobules-I-V

## Overview

The midline cerebellar vermal lobules I–V comprise the anterior portion of the cerebellar vermis, corresponding to the lingula, central lobule, and culmen in classical cerebellar nomenclature. These lobules form part of the spinocerebellum and receive extensive somatosensory input from the spinal cord, brainstem, and vestibular systems, contributing to axial posture, muscle tone, and coordination of midline and proximal limb movements. Histologically, they feature the typical trilaminar cerebellar cortical organization (molecular layer, Purkinje cell layer, and granular layer) overlying deep cerebellar nuclei, with a topographic representation of the body that is particularly enriched for trunk and proximal musculature. Functionally, lesions involving vermal lobules I–V can produce gait ataxia, truncal instability, and impaired balance, reflecting their role in integrating proprioceptive and vestibular signals for posture and locomotion.
