
# anterior-orbital-gyrus

## Overview

The left anterior orbital gyrus is a ventral prefrontal cortical region located on the orbital (inferior) surface of the frontal lobe, anteriorly within the orbitofrontal cortex. It lies above the orbits of the eyes, anterior to more posterior orbital gyri, and is involved in higher-order associative functions including value-based decision-making, reward and punishment processing, affective evaluation, and the integration of sensory and internal-state information to guide goal-directed behavior. Cytoarchitectonically, it is part of the heterogeneous orbitofrontal cortex, which is richly interconnected with limbic structures such as the amygdala, hippocampus, hypothalamus, and ventral striatum, as well as with other prefrontal and sensory association areas. Functionally, activity in the anterior orbital regions is frequently associated with emotional regulation, social cognition, and adaptive updating of behavioral strategies in response to changing environmental contingencies.
