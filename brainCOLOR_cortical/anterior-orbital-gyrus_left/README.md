
# anterior-orbital-gyrus

## Overview

The left anterior orbital gyrus is a cortical region located on the ventral (orbital) surface of the left frontal lobe, corresponding to the rostral portion of the orbitofrontal cortex that overlies the orbits of the eyes. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, it belongs to the frontal lobe parcellation of the orbitofrontal cortex and is associated with higher-order integrative functions such as reward evaluation, affective processing, and decision-making based on stimulus value. Cytoarchitectonically, it is part of the heterogeneous orbitofrontal cortex, which contains multiple granular and agranular fields interconnected with limbic structures (e.g., amygdala), sensory association cortices, and subcortical reward circuits. The left anterior orbital gyrus contributes to the integration of multimodal sensory information with internal states and prior experience to guide goal-directed behavior and flexible choice.
