
# precuneus

## Overview

The right precuneus is a medial parietal lobe structure located on the medial surface of the hemisphere, posterior to the paracentral lobule and anterior to the cuneus, bounded inferiorly by the subparietal sulcus. In the brainCOLOR atlas, it is defined as the right-hemispheric portion of the precuneus, corresponding primarily to Brodmann area 7 and adjacent medial parietal cortex. Anatomically, it is composed of association cortex receiving multimodal input from frontal, parietal, and occipital regions, and is richly interconnected with the posterior cingulate cortex, lateral parietal cortex, and medial prefrontal areas, forming a key hub of the default mode network. Functionally, the right precuneus is implicated in visuospatial processing, self-referential and episodic memory processes, aspects of consciousness, and integration of sensorimotor and cognitive information. There is no direct Wikipedia entry specifically for the “Right precuneus” region as defined in the brainCOLOR atlas; a closely related and encompassing structure is the precuneus: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precuneus.
