
# inferior-occipital-gyrus

## Overview

The left inferior occipital gyrus is a cortical region located on the basal and lateral surface of the occipital lobe, bordered superiorly by the lateral occipital gyri and anteriorly by the posterior portions of the temporal and fusiform gyri. It is part of the ventral visual processing stream and participates in early to intermediate stages of visual analysis, including form, object, and complex pattern processing, with strong connectivity to occipito-temporal regions such as the fusiform gyrus and the lateral occipital complex. Cytoarchitectonically, it belongs to the occipital association cortex, receiving dense input from primary and secondary visual areas and projecting to higher-order visual and multimodal regions. In functional imaging, activation of the left inferior occipital gyrus is frequently observed during tasks involving object recognition, reading, and processing of visually presented words and symbols, reflecting its role in transforming basic visual features into more abstract representations.
