
# parietal-operculum

## Overview

The left parietal operculum is a cortical region forming the dorsal component of the opercular rim overlying the insular cortex at the junction of the inferior parietal lobule and the posterior perisylvian area along the Sylvian fissure. It comprises portions of secondary somatosensory and multimodal association cortices involved in higher-order tactile processing, sensorimotor integration, and aspects of body representation and praxis, as well as contributing to speech-related sensorimotor functions through its connectivity with frontal opercular and temporal perisylvian regions. Cytoarchitectonically, the parietal operculum overlaps with areas OP1–OP4 and is bordered by the supramarginal gyrus, postcentral gyrus, and posterior insula, participating in bilateral networks for somatosensory discrimination, integration of somatic and vestibular inputs, and coordination of orofacial and hand movements.
