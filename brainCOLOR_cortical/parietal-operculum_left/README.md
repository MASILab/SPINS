
# parietal-operculum

## Overview

The left parietal operculum is a cortical region located along the dorsal portion of the Sylvian (lateral) fissure, forming part of the opercular covering over the insula and belonging to the inferior parietal lobule at the parietal–temporal junction. It comprises portions of secondary somatosensory and multimodal association cortices that participate in higher-order tactile processing, sensorimotor integration, and aspects of body representation, and is functionally connected with primary somatosensory cortex, premotor areas, and insular cortex. This region contributes to the integration of somatic sensations (such as touch, texture, and proprioception) with motor planning and may play roles in functions such as tactile object recognition and the integration of somatosensory input into peripersonal space representation.
