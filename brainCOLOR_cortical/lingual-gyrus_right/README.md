
# lingual-gyrus

## Overview

The right lingual gyrus is a ventral occipital–temporal cortical region situated on the medial aspect of the right hemisphere, extending from the calcarine sulcus posteriorly toward the collateral sulcus anteriorly. It contributes to early and intermediate stages of visual processing, including analysis of complex shapes, scenes, and patterns, and is frequently implicated in visual word form and symbol processing, color perception, and visuospatial integration. Functionally, the right lingual gyrus is associated with processing of visual context and imagery rather than language-dominant functions, which are more lateralized to left hemisphere homologues. It has dense connections with primary and secondary visual cortices, fusiform gyrus, and limbic–parahippocampal regions, supporting its role in linking perceptual visual information with memory and higher-order visual cognition.
