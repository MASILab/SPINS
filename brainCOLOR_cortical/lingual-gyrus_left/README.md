
# lingual-gyrus

## Overview

The left lingual gyrus is a ventromedial occipital lobe structure located on the inferomedial surface of the cerebral hemisphere, extending from the calcarine sulcus posteriorly toward the temporal lobe anteriorly. It is cytoarchitectonically associated primarily with visual association cortices and participates in higher-order visual processing, including analysis of complex shapes, visual word and letter form recognition, and aspects of scene and color perception; in the dominant (typically left) hemisphere, it is often implicated in reading and visual language tasks. The lingual gyrus receives input from primary visual cortex and projects to temporal and parietal association areas, integrating visual information with linguistic and mnemonic processes, and shows functional activation in tasks involving visual memory, encoding of complex visual stimuli, and semantic processing of written words.
