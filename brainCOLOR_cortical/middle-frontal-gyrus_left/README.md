
# middle-frontal-gyrus

## Overview

The left middle frontal gyrus is a cortical region of the frontal lobe located on the lateral surface of the hemisphere, anterior to the precentral gyrus and between the superior and inferior frontal gyri. It participates in higher-order executive functions, including working memory, planning, decision-making, and the top-down control of attention, and often shows left-lateralized involvement in language-related processes such as verbal working memory and aspects of syntactic processing. Cytoarchitectonically, it spans portions of the dorsolateral prefrontal cortex (notably areas corresponding to Brodmann areas 9 and 46) and is interconnected with parietal, temporal, and limbic regions forming large-scale cognitive control and frontoparietal networks. In the context of the brainCOLOR Atlas, it is defined as a left-hemisphere anatomical region used for standardized parcellation in neuroimaging analyses, supporting consistent labeling across studies focusing on structural and functional connectivity, task activation, and cortical morphology. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “Left middle-frontal-gyrus” as labeled in the brainCOLOR Atlas; a closely related and encompassing structure is the middle frontal gyrus: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_frontal_gyrus.
