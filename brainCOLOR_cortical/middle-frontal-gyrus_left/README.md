
# middle-frontal-gyrus

## Overview

The left middle frontal gyrus (MFG) is a dorsolateral prefrontal cortical region located in the frontal lobe, bounded inferiorly by the inferior frontal sulcus and superiorly by the superior frontal sulcus, extending anteriorly from the precentral sulcus toward the frontal pole. Cytoarchitectonically, it includes portions of Brodmann areas 9, 46, and related prefrontal fields, and is supplied primarily by branches of the middle cerebral artery. The left MFG is implicated in higher-order executive functions such as working memory, attentional control, cognitive flexibility, and aspects of language-related processing, including verbal working memory and controlled retrieval. It participates in large-scale brain networks such as the frontoparietal control network and exhibits extensive connectivity with parietal association cortex, premotor regions, basal ganglia, and thalamus, supporting the integration of sensory, mnemonic, and motor planning information necessary for goal-directed behavior.

There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for the “left middle frontal gyrus” as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas; a closely related and encompassing structure is the (bilateral) middle frontal gyrus: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_frontal_gyrus
