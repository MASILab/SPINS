
# superior-occipital-gyrus

## Overview

The left superior occipital gyrus is a dorsal occipital lobe region located superiorly along the lateral surface of the hemisphere, bordered by the intraparietal sulcus dorsally and other occipital gyri ventrally. It is primarily associated with higher-order visual processing, including aspects of visuospatial analysis, integration of motion and form information, and interaction with parietal regions involved in spatial attention and visually guided behavior. This gyrus participates in the dorsal visual stream (“where/how” pathway), contributing to the transformation of visual input into spatial representations that support orientation, navigation, and visually guided actions. Cytoarchitectonically, it lies within the association visual cortex (beyond primary visual area V1), and functionally it interacts with both occipital and posterior parietal regions in networks supporting visuospatial cognition and complex visual perception.
