
# superior-occipital-gyrus

## Overview

The left superior occipital gyrus is a dorsal occipital lobe structure situated superior to the calcarine sulcus and posterior to the parietal lobe, forming part of the higher-order visual association cortex. It contributes to visuospatial processing, motion perception, and integration of visual information with attentional and oculomotor systems, receiving input from primary visual areas and projecting to parietal and frontal regions involved in visual guidance of action. Cytoarchitectonically, it corresponds largely to portions of the extrastriate cortex (including parts of Brodmann areas such as 18/19), with predominantly contralateral visual field representation and strong interhemispheric connections through the splenium of the corpus callosum. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, it is defined as a distinct gyral label aligned to individual cortical folding patterns and used in structural and functional imaging analyses to study lateralized visual and attentional networks. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the left superior occipital gyrus; a related entry describing its broader cortical territory is the occipital lobe: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occipital_lobe.
