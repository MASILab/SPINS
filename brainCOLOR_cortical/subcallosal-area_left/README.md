
# subcallosal-area

## Overview

The left subcallosal area is a ventromedial prefrontal limbic region located beneath the genu of the corpus callosum in the medial frontal lobe, corresponding largely to Brodmann area 25 and often referred to as the subgenual cingulate cortex. It forms part of the medial prefrontal–cingulate network and is closely associated with adjacent structures such as the anterior cingulate cortex, septal area, and hypothalamus via dense reciprocal connections, including projections to and from the amygdala, hippocampus, and brainstem autonomic centers. Cytoarchitectonically, it is characterized by a granular to dysgranular cortical organization and is involved in affective, visceromotor, and autonomic regulation, playing a key role in mood, emotional valuation, and homeostatic control; abnormal structure or activity in this region has been strongly implicated in major depressive disorder and is a common target of deep brain stimulation for treatment-resistant depression.
