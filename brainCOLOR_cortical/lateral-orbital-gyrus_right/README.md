
# lateral-orbital-gyrus

## Overview

The right lateral orbital gyrus is a cortical region located on the inferior surface of the frontal lobe within the orbitofrontal cortex, overlying the orbital plate of the frontal bone and forming part of the prefrontal association cortex. It is involved in reward valuation, decision-making, emotion regulation, and the integration of sensory information (particularly olfactory and visceral signals) with affective and motivational states. Functionally, this area participates in evaluating the emotional significance of stimuli, updating stimulus–reward contingencies, and guiding adaptive behavioral responses, particularly in social and affective contexts. It is interconnected with limbic structures such as the amygdala and hippocampus, as well as other prefrontal regions, supporting its role in higher-order cognitive and emotional processes.
