
# supplementary-motor-cortex

## Overview

The Right supplementary-motor-cortex (right supplementary motor area, SMA) is a medial frontal lobe region located on the dorsomedial surface of the hemisphere, anterior to the primary motor cortex and within the superior frontal gyrus, largely corresponding to portions of Brodmann area 6. It plays a key role in the planning and initiation of voluntary movements, particularly in the sequencing and coordination of internally generated actions and in bimanual motor control. Neurons in this area are active prior to movement onset and are involved in motor preparation, motor imagery, and the temporal organization of complex motor programs. The right SMA contributes to higher-order aspects of motor behavior, including movement selection based on internal goals rather than external cues, and is functionally connected with primary motor cortex, premotor cortex, basal ganglia, and cerebellum, forming part of distributed motor control and sensorimotor integration networks. There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for the “Right supplementary-motor-cortex” as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas; a closely related and encompassing structure is described here: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supplementary_motor_area.
