
# posterior-cingulate-gyrus

## Overview

The left posterior cingulate gyrus is a medial cortical region located in the posterior part of the cingulate cortex, arching above the corpus callosum and forming part of the limbic lobe. It is heavily interconnected with the precuneus, medial prefrontal cortex, hippocampal formation, and lateral parietal areas, and is a key hub of the default mode network. Functionally, the left posterior cingulate gyrus is implicated in internally directed cognition, including autobiographical and episodic memory retrieval, self-referential processing, visuospatial imagery, and aspects of consciousness and awareness. It also participates in integrating emotional and cognitive information and shows altered activity or connectivity in disorders such as Alzheimer’s disease, depression, and certain neurodegenerative and psychiatric conditions. There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for the “left posterior cingulate gyrus” as a separate structure; a closely related and encompassing article is: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Posterior_cingulate.
