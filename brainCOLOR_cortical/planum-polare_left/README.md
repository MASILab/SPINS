
# planum-polare

## Overview

The left planum polare is a cortical region located anterior to Heschl’s gyrus on the superior temporal plane of the left temporal lobe, forming part of the auditory association cortex. It lies rostral to the planum temporale and is bounded laterally by the superior temporal gyrus, participating in higher-order processing of complex sounds, including aspects of speech and music perception. Cytoarchitectonically, it belongs to higher auditory areas rather than primary auditory cortex, with dense connections to adjacent temporal and frontal regions involved in language and auditory cognition. Within the brainCOLOR atlas, the left planum polare is a labeled anatomical subdivision of the superior temporal plane used for standardized parcellation in neuroimaging studies. There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for the “Left planum-polare”; a closely related and encompassing structure is the superior temporal gyrus: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superior_temporal_gyrus.
