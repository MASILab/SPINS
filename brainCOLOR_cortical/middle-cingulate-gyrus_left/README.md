
# middle-cingulate-gyrus

## Overview

The left middle cingulate gyrus is a portion of the cingulate cortex located on the medial surface of the cerebral hemisphere, dorsal to the corpus callosum and roughly corresponding to the mid-portion of the cingulate sulcus and its adjacent cortical ribbon. It forms part of the limbic–paralimbic system and is often functionally distinguished from anterior and posterior cingulate regions by its involvement in cognitive control, response selection, error monitoring, and the integration of motor, pain, and affective signals. Neurally, it participates in networks linking prefrontal executive areas, premotor and motor cortices, and subcortical structures such as the thalamus and basal ganglia, contributing to goal-directed behavior and the regulation of autonomic and affective components of action. There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for the “left middle-cingulate-gyrus” region; a closely related and encompassing structure is the cingulate cortex: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cingulate_cortex.
