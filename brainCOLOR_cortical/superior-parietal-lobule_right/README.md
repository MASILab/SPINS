
# superior-parietal-lobule

## Overview

The right superior parietal lobule is a dorsal parietal cortical region located on the lateral surface of the parietal lobe, situated posterior to the postcentral gyrus and superior to the intraparietal sulcus, and is typically bounded posteriorly by the superior occipital cortex. It comprises part of the posterior parietal association cortex and is heavily interconnected with primary and secondary somatosensory areas, premotor regions, and occipital visual cortices. Functionally, the right superior parietal lobule plays a central role in multimodal sensory integration, spatial attention, visuomotor coordination, and the internal representation of body schema, particularly in egocentric spatial reference frames. It contributes to the guidance of reaching and grasping movements, the integration of visual and proprioceptive information, and the allocation of attention in extrapersonal space; lesions in this region, especially in the right hemisphere, are associated with deficits such as hemispatial neglect and impairments in complex spatial processing.
