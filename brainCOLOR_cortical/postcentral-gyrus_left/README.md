
# postcentral-gyrus

## Overview

The left postcentral gyrus is a cortical region of the parietal lobe located immediately posterior to the central sulcus and constitutes the primary somatosensory cortex (Brodmann areas 3, 1, and 2) in the left hemisphere. It receives dense thalamocortical input from the ventral posterior nuclei of the thalamus and is organized somatotopically, forming part of the sensory homunculus that represents contralateral body regions, including face, upper limb, trunk, and lower limb. Neuronal populations within this gyrus process tactile, proprioceptive, and nociceptive information, supporting functions such as discrimination of touch, localization of stimuli on the body surface, and perception of limb position. The left postcentral gyrus also contributes to higher-order somatosensory integration and interacts with motor and premotor areas to guide sensorimotor coordination and skilled movement.
