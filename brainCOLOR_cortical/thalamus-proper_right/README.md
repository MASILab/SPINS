
# Thalamus-Proper

## Overview

The Right Thalamus-Proper, as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas, corresponds to the main bulk of thalamic gray matter in the right hemisphere, excluding specialized nuclei sometimes segmented separately (such as the lateral geniculate nucleus). The thalamus is a paired diencephalic structure composed of multiple nuclei that serve as a major relay and integration hub for sensory (excluding olfaction), motor, limbic, and associative information traveling between subcortical regions and the cerebral cortex. Functionally, thalamic nuclei participate in regulating arousal, attention, consciousness, and the gating of sensory input, as well as in coordinating cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical loops involved in movement and cognition. The Right Thalamus-Proper thus contributes to these processes in a largely bilateral and interconnected manner, with some lateralized functional specializations depending on cortical network organization.
