
# Thalamus-Proper

## Overview

The Right Thalamus-Proper, as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas, corresponds to the main mass of thalamic gray matter in the right hemisphere, excluding adjacent epithalamic and subthalamic structures. It is a paired, ovoid relay and integration center situated dorsal to the midbrain and lateral to the third ventricle, composed of multiple nuclei that process and transmit sensory, motor, limbic, and associative information to the cerebral cortex. Afferent inputs arise from the spinal cord, brainstem, basal ganglia, and cerebellum, while efferent projections are topographically organized to specific cortical regions, thereby contributing to sensory perception, arousal, attention, motor control, and aspects of cognition and emotion. Vascularization is primarily via branches of the posterior cerebral and posterior communicating arteries, and the region is clinically relevant in disorders such as thalamic stroke, neuropathic pain, movement disorders, and certain forms of epilepsy and coma.
