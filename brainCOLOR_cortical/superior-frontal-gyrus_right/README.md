
# superior-frontal-gyrus

## Overview

The right superior frontal gyrus is a dorsal frontal lobe structure extending along the superior frontal surface, anterior to the precentral gyrus and medial to the middle frontal gyrus, and is involved in higher-order cognitive and executive functions. It contributes to working memory, attention control, decision-making, and aspects of self-referential processing, and participates in networks supporting motor planning and voluntary action initiation. Cytoarchitectonically, it encompasses portions of premotor and prefrontal cortex, and is interconnected with other frontal regions, parietal association areas, and subcortical nuclei, forming part of large-scale cognitive control and default-mode networks. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, the “Right superior-frontal-gyrus” label corresponds to the right-hemispheric homolog of this region, segmented for structural and functional imaging analyses.
