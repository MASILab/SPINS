
# Cerebellar-Vermal-Lobules-VI-VII

## Overview

The Midline Cerebellar Vermal Lobules VI–VII correspond to a superior–posterior segment of the cerebellar vermis, located along the midline between the cerebellar hemispheres and forming part of the posterior lobe. These lobules receive predominantly spinocerebellar, pontocerebellar, and vestibulocerebellar inputs and project mainly to fastigial and interposed cerebellar nuclei, contributing to the modulation of posture, axial and proximal limb control, and the temporal coordination of movements. Functionally, vermal lobules VI–VII are also implicated in oculomotor control and higher-order processes such as attention and affect regulation, consistent with the broader “cerebellar cognitive–affective” network. In structural and functional imaging, this region is often grouped within the spinocerebellar and cognitive–affective territories of the vermis and shows involvement in disorders affecting coordination, gait, and emotional regulation.
