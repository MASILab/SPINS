
# planum-temporal

## Overview

The right planum temporale is a triangular cortical region located on the superior temporal plane, posterior to Heschl’s gyrus, forming part of the superior temporal lobe and overlying portions of the lateral sulcus. It is composed predominantly of granular and agranular neocortex (Brodmann areas 22 and adjacent regions), and is heavily interconnected with primary and secondary auditory cortices, multimodal association areas, and language-related networks. Functionally, the planum temporale is implicated in higher-order auditory processing, sound localization, phonological analysis, and aspects of language and music perception, with a well-known leftward structural asymmetry in most individuals that has been associated with hemispheric specialization for language. In the right hemisphere, the planum temporale is thought to contribute more to nonverbal auditory functions such as prosody, spectral-pitch analysis, and spatial hearing than to core linguistic decoding. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “right planum temporale” as a separate entity; a related structure with detailed coverage is the general planum temporale: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planum_temporale
