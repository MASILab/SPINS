
# medial-frontal-cortex

## Overview

The left medial frontal cortex is a region of the frontal lobe located on the medial (inner) surface of the left cerebral hemisphere, extending anteriorly from the supplementary motor area and pre-supplementary motor area toward the rostral and ventromedial prefrontal territories. It includes portions of Brodmann areas such as 6, 8, 9, 10, 24, and 32, depending on the parcellation scheme, and is interconnected with limbic, premotor, and prefrontal networks. Functionally, it is implicated in action selection, performance monitoring, decision-making, motivation, and aspects of self-referential processing, often overlapping with regions associated with the anterior cingulate cortex and medial prefrontal cortex. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, this region is defined as a label within a standardized parcellation system for structural and functional imaging analyses, emphasizing its anatomical boundaries and laterality within left frontal lobe networks. There is no direct Wikipedia page for “left medial frontal cortex” as a distinct entry; a closely related and encompassing structure is the medial prefrontal cortex: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medial_prefrontal_cortex.
