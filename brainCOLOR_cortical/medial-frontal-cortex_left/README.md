
# medial-frontal-cortex

## Overview

The left medial frontal cortex is a region of the frontal lobe located on the medial (inner) surface of the left hemisphere, extending anteriorly from the supplementary motor area and encompassing portions of the medial prefrontal cortex, anterior cingulate cortex, and adjacent medial aspects of the superior frontal gyrus. It is implicated in higher-order executive functions, including decision-making, performance monitoring, error detection, and the regulation of goal-directed behavior, as well as aspects of social cognition and emotional regulation. Functionally, this region integrates information about internal states, actions, and outcomes, contributing to motivation, cognitive control, and the selection and initiation of voluntary actions. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, it is treated as a functionally and anatomically coherent parcel within the broader medial prefrontal and cingulate network of the frontal lobe. There is no direct Wikipedia page for “Left medial-frontal-cortex” as labeled in the brainCOLOR Atlas; a closely related and encompassing structure is the medial prefrontal cortex: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medial_prefrontal_cortex
