
# orbital-part-of-the-IFG

## Overview

The left orbital part of the inferior frontal gyrus (left orbital-part-of-the-IFG) is a ventral subdivision of the inferior frontal gyrus located on the orbital surface of the frontal lobe, overlying the anterior cranial fossa and bordering the frontal pole and medial orbitofrontal cortex. Cytoarchitectonically, this region corresponds primarily to portions of Brodmann areas 47/12 and adjacent ventral BA45, characterized by granular prefrontal cortex with dense cortico-cortical connectivity to limbic, temporal, and other prefrontal areas. Functionally, the left orbital IFG is implicated in higher-order language processing (including semantic selection and controlled retrieval), integration of affective and reward-related information into decision-making, and aspects of inhibitory control and social cognition. Through its connections with anterior temporal cortex, amygdala, and medial prefrontal regions, it contributes to evaluation of emotional significance of stimuli and context-dependent modulation of behavior.
