
# Amygdala

## Overview

The right amygdala is a key subcortical structure within the medial temporal lobe, forming part of the limbic system and playing a central role in emotional processing, particularly in rapid detection and evaluation of threat-related stimuli, emotional memory, and modulation of autonomic and endocrine responses. Composed of multiple nuclei (including basolateral, centromedial, and cortical groups), it receives highly processed sensory input from cortical association areas and projects to the hypothalamus, brainstem, and prefrontal regions, thereby influencing fear conditioning, arousal, and decision-making under emotional load. The right amygdala is often associated with processing the emotional intensity and salience of stimuli, with some lateralization evidence suggesting a stronger role in fast, automatic responses to negative or fear-inducing cues compared with its left counterpart.
