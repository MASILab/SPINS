
# calcarine-cortex

## Overview

The right calcarine cortex is a region of the occipital lobe located along the right calcarine sulcus, forming a major portion of the primary visual cortex (V1, Brodmann area 17). It receives highly organized, retinotopic input from the lateral geniculate nucleus of the thalamus via the optic radiations, with the upper and lower banks of the calcarine sulcus representing the inferior and superior visual fields, respectively. Neurons in this region are specialized for processing basic visual features such as orientation, spatial frequency, contrast, and motion, and they serve as the initial cortical stage for transforming retinal signals into the perceptual representation of the contralateral (left) visual hemifield. The right calcarine cortex is essential for conscious vision, and lesions here can result in left homonymous visual field defects, including quadrantanopia or hemianopia, depending on lesion extent. There is no direct Wikipedia page for “Right calcarine cortex”; a closely related and encompassing structure is the primary visual cortex: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primary_visual_cortex.
