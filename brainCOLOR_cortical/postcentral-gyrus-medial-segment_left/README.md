
# postcentral-gyrus-medial-segment

## Overview

The left postcentral gyrus, medial segment, corresponds to the medial portion of the primary somatosensory cortex (S1) located in the postcentral gyrus of the left parietal lobe, adjacent to the midline along the longitudinal fissure. This region receives dense thalamocortical input from the ventral posterior nuclei of the thalamus and is somatotopically organized, with the medial segment predominantly representing sensory input from the lower limb, foot, and genital regions in the classic sensory homunculus. Neurons in this area are involved in processing modalities such as fine touch, proprioception, and vibration, contributing to conscious perception of body position and tactile discrimination. Cortical columns and layers within this region support integration of peripheral sensory information and relay to higher-order parietal association areas for further multimodal processing and sensorimotor integration.
