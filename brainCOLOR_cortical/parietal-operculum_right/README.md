
# parietal-operculum

## Overview

The right parietal operculum is a cortical region forming part of the opercular cover over the insula at the junction of the inferior parietal lobule and the lateral sulcus. Cytoarchitectonically, it spans portions of somatosensory association cortex (including secondary somatosensory cortex, S2, and adjacent parietal opercular areas) and participates in multimodal integration of tactile, proprioceptive, nociceptive, and vestibular information. Functionally, the right parietal operculum is implicated in higher-order somatosensory processing, body representation, sensorimotor integration, and aspects of spatial attention, and it interacts with frontal and insular regions as part of networks subserving perception of touch, pain, and bodily self-awareness.
