
# parietal-operculum

## Overview

The right parietal operculum is a cortical region forming part of the parietal lobe’s opercular surface, overlying portions of the insular cortex and bordering the lateral sulcus. It corresponds largely to the secondary somatosensory cortex (S2) and adjacent parietal opercular areas, receiving dense input from primary somatosensory regions and participating in higher-order processing of tactile, proprioceptive, and pain information, including bilateral integration and multimodal sensory representation. Functionally, it contributes to somatosensory discrimination, sensorimotor integration, body representation, and aspects of spatial attention, and is interconnected with the insula, primary somatosensory cortex, premotor regions, and subcortical nuclei.
