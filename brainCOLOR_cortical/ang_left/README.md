
# AnG

## Overview

The Left AnG (left angular gyrus) is a cortical region located in the inferior parietal lobule, at the junction of the parietal, temporal, and occipital lobes, and corresponds approximately to Brodmann area 39. It lies posterior to the supramarginal gyrus and inferior to the intraparietal sulcus, wrapping around the posterior end of the superior temporal sulcus. Cytoarchitectonically, it is characterized by heteromodal association cortex, integrating multimodal sensory inputs. Functionally, the left angular gyrus is implicated in language (reading, writing, semantic processing), number processing, spatial cognition, and aspects of theory of mind and episodic memory retrieval, with particular importance in the left hemisphere for language-dominant networks.
