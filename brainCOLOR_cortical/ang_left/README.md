
# AnG

## Overview

The Left AnG (left angular gyrus) is a cortical region located in the posterior part of the inferior parietal lobule, bordering the supramarginal gyrus anteriorly, the superior occipital cortex posteriorly, and the superior temporal gyrus ventrally. It is supplied primarily by branches of the middle cerebral artery and is cytoarchitectonically related to Brodmann area 39. Functionally, the left angular gyrus is implicated in language processing (including reading, writing, and semantic integration), number processing, spatial cognition, memory retrieval, and aspects of social cognition such as theory of mind. It serves as a multimodal association area, integrating visual, auditory, and somatosensory information, and participates in distributed networks supporting default-mode activity and high-level conceptual processing.

There is no direct Wikipedia link for the “Left AnG” label as used in the brainCOLOR Atlas; a closely related and encompassing structure is the angular gyrus: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angular_gyrus
