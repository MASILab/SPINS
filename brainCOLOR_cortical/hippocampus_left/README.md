
# Hippocampus

## Overview

The left hippocampus is a curved, seahorse-shaped structure located in the medial temporal lobe, forming part of the limbic system and lying within the hippocampal formation, which includes the dentate gyrus, CA fields (CA1–CA4), and subiculum. It receives highly processed multimodal sensory input via the entorhinal cortex and participates in the encoding, consolidation, and retrieval of declarative (especially verbal) memories, as well as in spatial navigation and contextual association, with a relative specialization for language-related and verbal episodic memory compared to the right hippocampus. Its circuitry is characterized by the trisynaptic pathway (entorhinal cortex → dentate gyrus → CA3 → CA1) and rich glutamatergic and GABAergic transmission, and it is notably vulnerable to hypoxia, stress, and neurodegenerative processes such as those seen in Alzheimer’s disease and temporal lobe epilepsy. There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for the “left hippocampus”; a closely related and encompassing structure is the hippocampus: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hippocampus_(brain).
