
# inferior-temporal-gyrus

## Overview

The left inferior temporal gyrus is a elongated cortical structure located on the inferolateral aspect of the left temporal lobe, lying between the inferior temporal sulcus superiorly and the occipitotemporal (collateral) sulcus inferiorly, and extending from the temporal pole anteriorly toward the occipital lobe posteriorly. It is composed primarily of neocortex and is interconnected with higher-order visual regions in the occipital and temporal lobes, as well as language- and memory-related areas in the temporal and frontal lobes. Functionally, the left inferior temporal gyrus participates in high-level visual processing, including object, face, and word-form recognition, and contributes to semantic processing and language-related visual recognition, especially in the dominant (usually left) hemisphere. It receives input from earlier visual areas in the ventral stream and projects to multimodal association cortices, supporting the integration of visual information with conceptual and linguistic representations.
