
# Thalamus-Proper

## Overview

The left thalamus-proper, as defined in the brainCOLOR atlas, refers to the main mass of thalamic gray matter in the left hemisphere, excluding neighboring nuclei that may be segmented separately in some parcellation schemes (such as the lateral and medial geniculate bodies). Biologically, the thalamus is a paired diencephalic structure composed predominantly of relay and association nuclei that receive, process, and transmit sensory, motor, and integrative information to the cerebral cortex via thalamocortical projections, while also participating in cortico-thalamo-cortical loops critical for consciousness, attention, and higher-order cognition. The left thalamus-proper contains nuclei involved in relaying somatosensory input from the contralateral side of the body, modulating motor signals (via connections with the basal ganglia and cerebellum), and integrating signals for language, memory, and executive functions through extensive connections with frontal, parietal, and temporal association cortices; its vascular supply is mainly through perforating branches of the posterior cerebral and posterior communicating arteries.
