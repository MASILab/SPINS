
# anterior-cingulate-gyrus

## Overview

The left anterior cingulate gyrus is a medial frontal lobe structure forming part of the cingulate cortex, arching above the corpus callosum and extending rostrally from the genu of the corpus callosum. It lies within the limbic system and is cytoarchitectonically associated with portions of Brodmann areas 24, 32, and 33. Functionally, the left anterior cingulate gyrus is implicated in cognitive control, error monitoring, conflict detection, affect regulation, and modulation of autonomic responses. It contributes to integration of emotional and cognitive information, particularly during decision-making, anticipation of reward or punishment, and regulation of mood and anxiety. Through dense connections with prefrontal, limbic (including amygdala), and subcortical structures (such as the thalamus and basal ganglia), it supports higher-order executive functions and the coordination of goal-directed behavior with internal emotional states.
