
# medial-orbital-gyrus

## Overview

The left medial orbital gyrus is a cortical region located on the ventromedial surface of the frontal lobe, forming part of the orbitofrontal cortex within the prefrontal cortex. It lies on the medial portion of the orbital surface, bordering the gyrus rectus and adjacent to the medial wall of the hemisphere above the orbits. Cytoarchitectonically, it contributes to higher-order association circuitry involved in reward valuation, decision-making, and emotional and social behavior, integrating limbic, sensory, and autonomic information. Its connections include reciprocal projections with other orbitofrontal areas, the medial prefrontal cortex, limbic regions such as the amygdala, and subcortical structures including the hypothalamus and striatum, supporting roles in affect regulation, motivation, and adaptive choice behavior.
