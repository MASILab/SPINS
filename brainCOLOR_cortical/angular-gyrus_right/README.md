
# angular-gyrus

## Overview

The right angular gyrus is a region of association cortex located in the posterior part of the inferior parietal lobule, wrapping around the posterior end of the superior temporal sulcus at the junction of the parietal, temporal, and occipital lobes. Cytoarchitectonically, it is often associated with Brodmann area 39. This region is implicated in multimodal integration of visual, auditory, and somatosensory information, and is involved in higher-order cognitive processes including spatial attention, number processing, language comprehension (especially semantic processing), theory of mind, and aspects of episodic memory retrieval. Lesions in the right angular gyrus have been associated with deficits such as hemispatial neglect and disturbances in spatial cognition and awareness.
