
# anterior-insula

## Overview

The right anterior insula is the rostral portion of the insular cortex located deep within the lateral sulcus of the right cerebral hemisphere, overlaid by the frontal, parietal, and temporal opercula. Cytoarchitectonically agranular to dysgranular, it forms part of the paralimbic cortex and is heavily interconnected with the anterior cingulate cortex, prefrontal cortex, amygdala, thalamus, and brainstem autonomic centers. Functionally, the right anterior insula is implicated in interoceptive awareness, autonomic regulation, emotional processing (especially negative affect and anxiety), salience detection, and integration of internal bodily states with cognitive and social-emotional processes. It also plays a key role in the salience network, contributing to switching between default mode and central executive networks.
