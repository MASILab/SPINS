
# anterior-insula

## Overview

The right anterior insula is the rostral portion of the insular cortex located deep within the lateral sulcus of the right hemisphere, overlaid by the frontal and temporal opercula. Cytoarchitectonically agranular-to-dysgranular, it is extensively interconnected with the anterior cingulate cortex, orbitofrontal cortex, amygdala, and brainstem autonomic centers, and is a core node of the salience network. Functionally, the right anterior insula is implicated in interoceptive awareness (representation of internal bodily states such as heartbeat and visceral signals), integration of emotional and cognitive information, subjective feeling states, risk and uncertainty processing, and initiating switches between default mode and executive control networks. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, it is defined as a discrete parcellation within the insular cortex rather than as a gross anatomical lobe. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “right anterior insula” as labeled in the brainCOLOR Atlas; a closely related structure and encompassing region is the insular cortex: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insular_cortex
