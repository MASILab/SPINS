
# Basal-Forebrain

## Overview

The left basal forebrain is a ventral telencephalic region located anterior to the hypothalamus and below the anterior commissure, encompassing nuclei such as the nucleus basalis of Meynert, medial septal nucleus, diagonal band nuclei, and adjacent ventral pallidal areas that provide widespread cholinergic, GABAergic, and glutamatergic projections to the cerebral cortex, hippocampus, and amygdala, thereby modulating arousal, attention, learning, memory, and sleep–wake regulation; in the brainCOLOR atlas, the “Left Basal-Forebrain” label denotes the left-hemispheric portion of this complex, which is cytoarchitectonically heterogeneous and highly interconnected with limbic and prefrontal circuits, and is notably vulnerable to degenerative processes such as those occurring in Alzheimer’s disease and other dementias. There is no direct Wikipedia page for “Left Basal-Forebrain” as a brainCOLOR structure; a closely related entry is the general basal forebrain article: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basal_forebrain
