
# middle-occipital-gyrus

## Overview

The right middle occipital gyrus (MOG) is a lateral occipital lobe structure involved primarily in intermediate- to high-level visual processing, including analysis of object form, motion, and spatial features. Situated between the superior and inferior occipital gyri, it receives prominent input from early visual cortices and participates in the dorsal and ventral visual streams, contributing to the integration of visual information for perception and visually guided behavior. The right hemisphere localization is associated with visuospatial processing and may be particularly important for global visual patterns and spatial attention. Functionally, the right MOG is engaged during tasks involving visual recognition, mental imagery, motion perception, and attention to visual stimuli, and it shows robust activation in functional neuroimaging studies of visual cognition.
