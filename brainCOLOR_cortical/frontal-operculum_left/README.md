
# frontal-operculum

## Overview

The left frontal operculum is a cortical region located on the ventrolateral surface of the left frontal lobe, forming part of the opercular cortex that overlies the insula and contributes to the inferior frontal gyrus. It is bordered by portions of the precentral gyrus posteriorly and the pars triangularis and pars orbitalis anteriorly and ventrally, and it participates in a range of functions including language production, speech motor planning, and higher-order cognitive control. In many parcellation schemes, including the brainCOLOR Atlas, this region is treated as a distinct anatomical and functional subdivision within the inferior frontal cortex, often overlapping with or adjacent to areas classically associated with Broca’s region. The left frontal operculum is heavily interconnected with temporal and parietal language areas, the insula, and subcortical nuclei, supporting integrative roles in articulatory control and multimodal processing.
