
# frontal-operculum

## Overview

The left frontal operculum is a cortical region forming part of the ventrolateral frontal lobe, overlying the insula and bounded by parts of the inferior frontal gyrus and adjacent peri-Sylvian cortex. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, it is classified as a distinct opercular parcel within the frontal lobe, contributing to higher-order language, speech-motor planning, and cognitive control processes, and functionally overlapping in part with regions associated with Broca’s area in the dominant (usually left) hemisphere. The frontal operculum participates in integrating articulatory, phonological, and syntactic operations with executive functions, and is structurally characterized by its location along the Sylvian fissure, where frontal, parietal, and temporal opercula converge to cover the insular cortex. There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for the “Left frontal-operculum” as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas; a related and encompassing structure is described here: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frontal_operculum.
