
# Caudate

## Overview

The right caudate is the right-sided portion of the caudate nucleus, a C-shaped gray matter structure deep within the telencephalon and a major component of the dorsal striatum in the basal ganglia. It lies adjacent to the lateral ventricle, with its head in the anterior horn, body along the lateral wall of the lateral ventricle, and tail extending into the temporal lobe toward the amygdala. Composed predominantly of medium spiny GABAergic neurons, the right caudate receives dense glutamatergic input from frontal and associative cortical areas and dopaminergic projections from the substantia nigra pars compacta. It plays key roles in motor control, action selection, procedural and habit learning, reward-based decision-making, and aspects of cognition such as working memory and executive function. Dysfunction or degeneration of the caudate, including the right caudate, is implicated in movement disorders (e.g., Huntington’s disease, Parkinson’s disease), obsessive–compulsive disorder, attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder, and various neuropsychiatric conditions.
