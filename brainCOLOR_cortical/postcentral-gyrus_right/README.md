
# postcentral-gyrus

## Overview

The right postcentral gyrus is a cortical region of the parietal lobe located immediately posterior to the central sulcus and constitutes the primary somatosensory cortex (Brodmann areas 3, 1, and 2) of the right hemisphere. It receives and integrates somatosensory input from the contralateral (left) side of the body via thalamocortical projections, including touch, proprioception, vibration, pressure, and nociceptive information, topographically organized in a somatotopic “sensory homunculus.” This region is critical for the conscious perception and fine discrimination of tactile stimuli, spatial localization of sensory inputs, and sensorimotor integration that supports skilled movements. Lesions in the right postcentral gyrus can lead to contralateral sensory deficits, such as impaired tactile discrimination, astereognosis, or hemisensory loss, and may contribute to disturbances in body representation and spatial awareness.
