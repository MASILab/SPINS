
# calcarine-cortex

## Overview

The left calcarine cortex is the portion of primary visual cortex (V1) situated along the calcarine sulcus in the medial aspect of the left occipital lobe, forming a key part of the striate cortex that receives and processes highly organized retinotopic input from the lateral geniculate nucleus of the thalamus. It is characterized by a prominent granular layer IV, dense myelination forming the line of Gennari, and a columnar and laminar organization that supports parallel processing of visual features such as orientation, spatial frequency, contrast, and basic motion. The upper and lower banks of the calcarine sulcus represent different portions of the contralateral visual field, with posterior regions encoding foveal vision and more anterior regions encoding peripheral vision. Functionally, the left calcarine cortex predominantly subserves visual processing for the right hemifield and serves as the primary cortical entry point for visual information that is subsequently relayed to extrastriate visual areas for higher-order processing.
