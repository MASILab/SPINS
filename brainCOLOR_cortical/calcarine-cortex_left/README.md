
# calcarine-cortex

## Overview

The left calcarine cortex is a region of the occipital lobe centered around the calcarine sulcus and corresponds largely to the primary visual cortex (V1, Brodmann area 17) in the left hemisphere. It receives highly organized visual input from the lateral geniculate nucleus of the thalamus via the optic radiations, with a retinotopic organization such that the central visual field is represented posteriorly and the peripheral field more anteriorly, and the upper and lower visual fields mapped to opposite banks of the calcarine sulcus. Neurons in this area are specialized for the initial cortical processing of basic visual features such as orientation, spatial frequency, contrast, and motion direction, providing the foundation for higher-order visual processing in extrastriate areas. Lesions of the left calcarine cortex typically cause right homonymous visual field defects (e.g., right homonymous hemianopia), often with macular sparing depending on vascular supply patterns from the posterior cerebral artery.
