
# Amygdala

## Overview

The left amygdala is an almond-shaped collection of nuclei located in the medial temporal lobe of the left cerebral hemisphere, anterior to the hippocampus and adjacent to the temporal horn of the lateral ventricle. It is a key component of the limbic system, heavily interconnected with the prefrontal cortex, hippocampal formation, hypothalamus, and sensory association cortices. Functionally, the left amygdala is involved in emotional processing, particularly in the evaluation and encoding of emotionally salient stimuli, fear conditioning, modulation of memory consolidation, and aspects of social cognition. It plays a critical role in autonomic and endocrine responses to stress via projections to hypothalamic and brainstem centers and participates in integrating sensory, contextual, and affective information to guide adaptive behavioral responses. There is no direct Wikipedia page for “Left amygdala” as a separate structure; a closely related and encompassing article is: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amygdala
