
# Amygdala

## Overview

The left amygdala is an almond-shaped collection of nuclei located in the medial temporal lobe, anterior to the hippocampus and adjacent to the temporal horn of the lateral ventricle. It is a key component of the limbic system and is critically involved in emotional processing, particularly in the detection and learning of biologically salient stimuli, including fear and reward-related cues. The left amygdala contributes to associative learning, modulation of autonomic and endocrine responses, encoding of emotional memories, and influences higher-order cognitive functions such as decision-making and social behavior through extensive reciprocal connections with the prefrontal cortex, hippocampus, sensory association cortices, and hypothalamus.
