
# fusiform-gyrus

## Overview

The left fusiform gyrus is a ventral temporal lobe structure located on the basal surface of the cerebral hemisphere, bounded laterally by the occipitotemporal sulcus and medially by the collateral sulcus, extending from the posterior occipital region to the anterior temporal lobe. It is cytoarchitectonically heterogeneous, encompassing regions implicated in high-level visual processing, including category-selective areas for faces (e.g., fusiform face area), written words (visual word form area), and other complex objects. The left fusiform gyrus plays a critical role in orthographic processing, lexical access, and integration of visual input with language systems, and is interconnected with occipital visual cortices, inferior temporal areas, and perisylvian language networks. Lesions in this region can result in deficits such as pure alexia or category-specific recognition impairments, depending on the subregion affected and its connectivity.
