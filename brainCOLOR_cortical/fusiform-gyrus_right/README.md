
# fusiform-gyrus

## Overview

The right fusiform gyrus is a ventral temporal lobe structure located on the basal surface of the cerebral hemisphere, extending longitudinally between the inferior temporal gyrus laterally and the parahippocampal gyrus medially, delineated by the occipitotemporal sulcus laterally and the collateral sulcus medially. It is composed primarily of association cortex and participates in high-level visual processing, including object, face, and word-form recognition, with the right hemisphere particularly implicated in holistic face perception and visual expertise for complex stimuli. The region receives input from earlier visual areas in the occipital lobe and projects to distributed networks in temporal and frontal cortices involved in semantic processing, memory, and social cognition. In the brainCOLOR atlas, “Right fusiform gyrus” denotes the right-hemispheric counterpart of this anatomically defined cortical territory. There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for “Right fusiform gyrus” as a standalone entry; a closely related and encompassing structure is the fusiform gyrus: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fusiform_gyrus
