
# medial-frontal-cortex

## Overview

The right medial frontal cortex is a region of the frontal lobe located on the medial (inner) surface of the right hemisphere, extending anteriorly from the supplementary motor area toward the frontal pole and encompassing parts of the medial prefrontal cortex and adjacent cingulate territory, depending on the specific parcellation used in the brainCOLOR Atlas. Biologically, this area participates in higher-order cognitive and affective functions, including action selection, performance monitoring, decision-making, and aspects of social cognition and self-referential processing, via dense connections with other prefrontal regions, limbic structures (such as the anterior cingulate cortex and amygdala), and subcortical nuclei (including basal ganglia and thalamus). Cytoarchitectonically, it typically includes agranular and dysgranular frontal fields that integrate multimodal sensory input with internal motivational and contextual signals to guide goal-directed behavior. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “right medial frontal cortex” as a standalone entry; a closely related and encompassing structure is the medial prefrontal cortex: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medial_prefrontal_cortex.
