
# medial-frontal-cortex

## Overview

The right medial frontal cortex is a region of the frontal lobe located on the medial (inner) surface of the right hemisphere, extending anteriorly from the supplementary motor areas toward the frontal pole and encompassing parts of medial Brodmann areas such as 8, 9, 10, 24, and 32, depending on the parcellation scheme. It lies superior to the cingulate gyrus and is involved in higher-order cognitive and affective functions, including decision-making, performance monitoring, error detection, response selection, and aspects of motivation and social cognition. Functionally, this region participates in large-scale networks such as the default mode network and cognitive control networks, integrating internal states with goal-directed behavior, and is often implicated in neuropsychiatric conditions such as depression, anxiety, and obsessive-compulsive disorder. There is no direct Wikipedia page for “Right medial frontal cortex”; a related and encompassing structure is the medial frontal gyrus: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medial_frontal_gyrus
