
# central-operculum

## Overview

The left central operculum is a cortical region forming part of the opercular covering of the insula, located along the lateral surface of the hemisphere, overlying the central sulcus and adjacent peri-Rolandic areas. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, it corresponds to opercular cortex situated between frontal and parietal opercular fields and extending over portions of the precentral and postcentral gyri that border the Sylvian (lateral) fissure. This region participates in sensorimotor integration and orofacial control, including aspects of articulation and somatosensory processing of the face and tongue, and is structurally connected with primary motor and somatosensory cortices, insular cortex, and perisylvian language-related areas. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “left central operculum” as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas; a related entry is the general opercular region: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operculum_(brain).
