
# central-operculum

## Overview

The left central operculum is a cortical region forming part of the opercular covering of the insula within the frontal and parietal lobes, located along the lateral surface of the cerebral hemisphere overlying the lateral sulcus (Sylvian fissure). In the brainCOLOR atlas, it corresponds to the portion of cortex surrounding the central sulcus in the opercular area, integrating aspects of primary somatosensory, motor, and multimodal association processing involved in orofacial sensorimotor functions, speech articulation, and integration of tactile and proprioceptive input from the face and mouth. It lies adjacent to regions of the inferior frontal gyrus and inferior parietal lobule that contribute to perisylvian language networks and is structurally related to the insular cortex, which it partially covers. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “left central operculum” as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas; a closely related and encompassing structure is the opercular region around the insula: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operculum_(brain)
