
# frontal-pole

## Overview

The left frontal pole is the most anterior portion of the left frontal lobe, corresponding approximately to Brodmann area 10, and is involved in high-level executive and metacognitive functions, including prospective memory, complex decision-making, multi-task coordination, and integration of internal goals with external stimuli. Cytoarchitectonically, it is characterized by a highly developed granular prefrontal cortex with extensive reciprocal connections to other prefrontal regions, the anterior cingulate cortex, parietal association areas, and limbic structures such as the hippocampus and amygdala, supporting its role in planning, abstract reasoning, and regulation of emotion and behavior over extended time scales. This region is functionally lateralized in some individuals, with the left frontal pole often implicated in language-related executive processes and verbal working memory, although its connectivity patterns indicate participation in distributed large-scale networks rather than strictly localized functions. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “left frontal-pole” as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas; a closely related and encompassing structure is the frontal lobe: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frontal_lobe.
