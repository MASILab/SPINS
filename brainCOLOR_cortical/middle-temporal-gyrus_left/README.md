
# middle-temporal-gyrus

## Overview

The left middle temporal gyrus (MTG) is a longitudinal cortical region located on the lateral surface of the left temporal lobe, situated between the superior and inferior temporal gyri and extending from the temporal pole posteriorly toward the temporo-parietal junction. Cytoarchitectonically, it encompasses portions of Brodmann areas 21 and 37 and participates in large-scale networks subserving language, semantic memory, and higher-order auditory and visual processing. Functionally, the left MTG is strongly implicated in lexical-semantic retrieval, sentence-level comprehension, and the integration of multimodal information, and it shows robust activation during tasks involving word meaning, narrative processing, and conceptual knowledge. It receives input from primary and higher-order auditory regions and projects to frontal and parietal association cortices, supporting its role as an associative hub in the language-dominant hemisphere. A stroke or lesion involving the left middle temporal gyrus can contribute to language deficits such as fluent (Wernicke-type) aphasia, impaired word comprehension, and semantic paraphasias, reflecting its importance in mapping acoustic and visual inputs onto stored semantic representations.
