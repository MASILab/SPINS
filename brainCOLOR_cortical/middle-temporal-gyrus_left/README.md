
# middle-temporal-gyrus

## Overview

The left middle temporal gyrus (MTG) is a longitudinal cortical region of the temporal lobe situated between the superior and inferior temporal gyri, extending from the temporal pole posteriorly toward the angular gyrus. It is primarily composed of association cortex and participates in higher-order auditory and multimodal processing, including semantic language processing, lexical access, and aspects of social cognition such as theory-of-mind and biological motion perception. The left MTG is strongly left-lateralized for language functions, forming part of the dorsal and ventral language networks that connect temporal regions with frontal language areas (e.g., inferior frontal gyrus) via white-matter pathways such as the arcuate and inferior fronto-occipital fasciculi. Cytoarchitectonically, it spans portions of Brodmann areas 21 and 22, receiving input from primary and secondary auditory cortices and projecting to widespread association regions, thereby contributing to the integration of auditory, visual, and conceptual information necessary for comprehension and contextual interpretation of speech and other complex stimuli.
