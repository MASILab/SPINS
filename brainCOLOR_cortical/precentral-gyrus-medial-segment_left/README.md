
# precentral-gyrus-medial-segment

## Overview

The left precentral gyrus medial segment is the medial portion of the primary motor cortex (Brodmann area 4) located on the dorsal-medial aspect of the frontal lobe, anterior to the central sulcus and bordering the medial longitudinal fissure. In the brainCOLOR atlas, this segment corresponds to the medial extension of the precentral gyrus that curves onto the mesial surface and includes part of the paracentral lobule, which contains motor representations of the lower limb and trunk. Cytoarchitectonically characterized by large pyramidal Betz cells in layer V, this region is a major origin of the corticospinal and corticobulbar tracts and is crucial for voluntary initiation and execution of contralateral leg and proximal body movements, as well as for motor aspects of posture and gait. There is no direct Wikipedia page for “Left precentral-gyrus-medial-segment”; a closely related structure is the precentral gyrus: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precentral_gyrus
