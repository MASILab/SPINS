
# Putamen

## Overview

The left putamen is the left-sided component of the putamen, a large, rounded nucleus located lateral to the globus pallidus and medial to the insular cortex within the basal ganglia of the telencephalon. Composed primarily of medium spiny GABAergic neurons, it receives dense excitatory glutamatergic input from the cerebral cortex (especially motor, premotor, and somatosensory areas) and thalamus, and modulatory dopaminergic input from the substantia nigra pars compacta. Functionally, the left putamen participates in motor planning and execution, habit formation, reinforcement learning, and aspects of procedural memory, integrating cortical signals and influencing movement via basal ganglia–thalamocortical loops. It is implicated in movement disorders such as Parkinson’s disease and Huntington’s disease, as well as certain neuropsychiatric conditions, through alterations in its connectivity, neurochemistry, or structural integrity.
