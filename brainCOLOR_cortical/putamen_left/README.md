
# Putamen

## Overview

The left putamen is the left-sided component of the putamen, a large, rounded nucleus within the dorsal striatum of the basal ganglia, situated lateral to the globus pallidus and medial to the insular cortex, separated from the caudate nucleus by the internal capsule. Composed predominantly of medium spiny GABAergic neurons, the left putamen receives dense excitatory glutamatergic inputs from the ipsilateral cerebral cortex, particularly motor, premotor, and somatosensory areas, as well as modulatory dopaminergic projections from the substantia nigra pars compacta. Together with the caudate nucleus, it participates in cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical loops that regulate motor planning, execution, habit formation, and aspects of procedural learning. Functional and structural asymmetries between left and right putamen have been reported in relation to language, motor dominance, and certain neuropsychiatric conditions, although the fundamental cytoarchitecture is largely bilateral. Degeneration or dysfunction of the left putamen is implicated in movement disorders such as Parkinson’s disease, Huntington’s disease, and dystonia, as well as in some cognitive and affective disturbances through its connectivity with frontal and limbic circuits.
