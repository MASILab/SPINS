
# frontal-pole

## Overview

The right frontal pole is the most anterior portion of the right frontal lobe, corresponding approximately to Brodmann area 10, and in the brainCOLOR Atlas is defined as a distinct cortical region at the rostral tip of the hemisphere. It is composed of granular prefrontal cortex with extensive connections to other prefrontal territories, limbic structures (including the anterior cingulate cortex and amygdala), and association cortex in parietal and temporal lobes. Functionally, this region is implicated in high-level cognitive processes such as multi-tasking, prospective memory, complex decision-making, and the integration of internal goals with external stimuli, as well as aspects of social cognition. Structurally, it lies anterior to the superior and middle frontal gyri and overlies the frontal pole of the underlying white matter tracts that project to widespread cortical and subcortical targets. There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for the “right frontal-pole” region as defined by the brainCOLOR Atlas; a closely related structure is “Frontal pole”: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frontal_pole.
