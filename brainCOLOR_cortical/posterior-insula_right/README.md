
# posterior-insula

## Overview

The right posterior insula is the caudal segment of the insular cortex located deep within the lateral sulcus of the right hemisphere, covered by portions of the frontal, parietal, and temporal opercula. Cytoarchitectonically, it is generally more granular than the anterior insula and is often associated with primary interoceptive and somatosensory processing, including representation of pain, temperature, visceral sensations, and vestibular information. Functionally, the right posterior insula contributes to integrating bodily states with sensorimotor and autonomic responses, forming part of a broader interoceptive network that interfaces with parietal, somatosensory, and limbic structures. There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for the “right posterior insula” as an isolated region; a closely related and encompassing structure is the insular cortex: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insular_cortex
