
# posterior-insula

## Overview

The right posterior insula is the caudal portion of the insular cortex located deep within the lateral sulcus of the right cerebral hemisphere, forming part of the paralimbic cortex and overlaid by the opercula of the frontal, parietal, and temporal lobes. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, it is delineated as a distinct region within the insular subdivision scheme, corresponding to posterior granular and dysgranular insular fields. Cytoarchitectonically, this area is characterized by a more pronounced granular layer IV relative to anterior insula, and it maintains strong connectivity with primary and secondary somatosensory cortices, vestibular regions, and interoceptive pathways from the thalamus and brainstem. Functionally, the right posterior insula is implicated in the representation of bodily states, including pain, temperature, visceral sensations, and vestibular processing, contributing to sensorimotor integration and interoceptive awareness that inform higher-order affective and cognitive processes in more anterior insular territories.
