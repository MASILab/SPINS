
# middle-cingulate-gyrus

## Overview

The right middle cingulate gyrus is a medial cortical region located within the cingulate cortex, extending along the dorsal aspect of the cingulate sulcus in the right cerebral hemisphere, and is often grouped functionally and cytoarchitectonically within the mid-cingulate cortex. It serves as an integrative hub linking motor, cognitive, and affective processes, with dense connections to the supplementary motor area, premotor cortex, dorsolateral prefrontal cortex, parietal association areas, and limbic structures such as the insula and amygdala. Neuronal populations in this region contribute to response selection, cognitive control, error monitoring, and motivated behavior, as well as the modulation of pain and negative affect through its role in affective-motivational components of nociception. The right middle cingulate gyrus participates in lateralized functions including aspects of visuo-spatial attention and autonomic regulation, and is frequently implicated in neuroimaging studies of effortful task performance, conflict monitoring, and chronic pain syndromes.
