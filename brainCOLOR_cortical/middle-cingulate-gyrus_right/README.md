
# middle-cingulate-gyrus

## Overview

The right middle cingulate gyrus is a subdivision of the cingulate cortex located on the medial aspect of the right cerebral hemisphere, dorsal to the corpus callosum and intermediate between the anterior and posterior cingulate regions. In the brainCOLOR atlas, it is defined as part of the mid-cingulate region, which is associated with cognitive control, response selection, error monitoring, and aspects of pain processing and motor control, including integration of motivational and motor-related signals. Cytoarchitectonically, it overlaps with portions of Brodmann areas 24 and 32 (and sometimes 23, depending on parcellation), and it forms part of large-scale networks such as the salience and cingulo-opercular control networks. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “right middle cingulate gyrus” as a separate entry; a related and encompassing structure is the cingulate cortex: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cingulate_cortex
