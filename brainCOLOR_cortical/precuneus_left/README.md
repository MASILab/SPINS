
# precuneus

## Overview

The left precuneus is a medial parietal lobe structure located on the medial surface of the hemisphere, bounded anteriorly by the marginal branch of the cingulate sulcus, posteriorly by the parieto-occipital sulcus, and inferiorly by the subparietal sulcus, corresponding to the medial portion of the superior parietal lobule. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, the left precuneus is delineated as a cortical region involved in higher-order associative functions, including visuospatial integration, mental imagery, self-referential processing, and aspects of episodic memory retrieval, and it forms part of the default mode network with strong connectivity to medial prefrontal, posterior cingulate, and lateral parietal cortices. Cytoarchitectonically, it encompasses subdivisions that may correspond to Brodmann areas 7 and adjacent territories, with dense corticocortical and thalamocortical inputs supporting multimodal integration. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “left precuneus” as a separate entry; a closely related and encompassing structure is described here: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precuneus.
