
# posterior-orbital-gyrus

## Overview

The left posterior orbital gyrus, as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas, is a cortical region located on the ventral (orbital) surface of the frontal lobe, lying posteriorly within the orbital gyri complex just anterior to the olfactory sulcus and above the orbital surface of the frontal bone. It forms part of the orbitofrontal cortex, a key area involved in reward processing, value-based decision-making, emotional evaluation, and integration of sensory information (particularly olfactory and gustatory input) with affective and motivational states. This region is connected with limbic structures such as the amygdala and hippocampus, as well as other prefrontal and sensory association areas, contributing to the modulation of behavior based on expected outcomes and social context.
