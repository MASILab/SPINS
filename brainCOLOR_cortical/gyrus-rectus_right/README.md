
# gyrus-rectus

## Overview

The right gyrus rectus, also known as the straight gyrus, is a medial orbital frontal lobe structure located on the inferior surface of the right frontal lobe, bordering the longitudinal fissure and lying medial to the medial orbital gyrus. It is part of the prefrontal cortex and is supplied primarily by branches of the anterior cerebral artery. Cytoarchitectonically, it is classified as prefrontal association cortex and has extensive connections with limbic and paralimbic regions, including the cingulate cortex and olfactory structures, supporting roles in higher-order cognitive processes, emotion, and aspects of social and reward-related behavior. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, the “Right gyrus-rectus” label corresponds to this right-hemispheric orbital frontal region; functionally and anatomically it is typically considered homologous to the left gyrus rectus and is often studied in the context of orbitofrontal circuits, traumatic brain injury, and neuropsychiatric disorders.
