
# gyrus-rectus

## Overview

The right gyrus rectus is the medial-most convolution of the orbitofrontal cortex on the inferior surface of the frontal lobe, bordering the longitudinal fissure and lying anterior to the optic chiasm and olfactory structures. It is cytoarchitectonically part of the prefrontal cortex and is implicated in higher-order cognitive and affective processes, including value-based decision-making, emotional regulation, and aspects of social cognition. The gyrus rectus has strong connections with limbic and paralimbic regions such as the anterior cingulate cortex, amygdala, and medial prefrontal areas, and is often examined in studies of mood disorders, obsessive-compulsive disorder, and neurodegenerative diseases where orbitofrontal and medial frontal atrophy or dysfunction are present. In the brainCOLOR atlas, “Right gyrus-rectus” denotes the right-hemispheric counterpart of this structure, used as a parcellated region of interest for structural and functional imaging analyses. There is no direct Wikipedia page for “Right gyrus-rectus” as a standalone entry; a related and encompassing structure is described here: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medial_frontal_gyrus.
