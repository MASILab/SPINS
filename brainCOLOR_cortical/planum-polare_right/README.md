
# planum-polare

## Overview

The right planum polare is a cortical region located anterior to Heschl’s gyrus on the superior temporal gyrus, forming part of the temporal lobe’s superior surface and belonging to the auditory association cortex. It is implicated in higher-order auditory processing, including analysis of complex sounds such as speech and music, and shows functional specialization in aspects of spectral and temporal pattern processing. Cytoarchitectonically, it lies adjacent to primary auditory cortex but is considered non-primary auditory cortex, integrating multimodal inputs and contributing to auditory object recognition and possibly language-related functions, particularly in concert with neighboring perisylvian areas. There is no direct Wikipedia article dedicated specifically to the right planum polare; a closely related structure and encompassing region is the planum temporale: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planum_temporale
