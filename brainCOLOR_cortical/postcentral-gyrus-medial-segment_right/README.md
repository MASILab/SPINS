
# postcentral-gyrus-medial-segment

## Overview

The right postcentral gyrus medial segment, as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas, corresponds to the medial portion of the primary somatosensory cortex located on the posterior bank of the central sulcus in the right cerebral hemisphere, extending toward the midline and often including cortex near the paracentral lobule. This region is primarily involved in the processing of somatosensory information from the contralateral (left) side of the body, with a strong representation of the lower limb and trunk according to the somatotopic organization of the sensory homunculus. Neurons in this area receive input from the ventral posterior nuclei of the thalamus and are critical for the conscious perception and discrimination of tactile stimuli, proprioception, and aspects of nociception. Functionally, the right medial postcentral gyrus integrates sensory information necessary for body schema, spatial localization of stimuli, and coordination with motor regions for sensorimotor control, particularly for lower limb posture and movement.
