
# anterior-orbital-gyrus

## Overview

The right anterior orbital gyrus is a cortical region located on the ventral (inferior) surface of the frontal lobe within the orbitofrontal cortex, overlying the orbits of the eyes. It is part of the orbital gyri complex, which is involved in reward valuation, decision-making, emotional processing, and the integration of sensory and visceral signals to guide adaptive behavior. Cytoarchitectonically, this region corresponds to portions of the orbitofrontal cortex that receive multimodal input from sensory association areas, limbic structures (such as the amygdala), and subcortical reward pathways, and it projects to widespread cortical and subcortical targets including the striatum. Functionally, activity in this region has been associated with evaluation of outcomes, processing of social and emotional cues, and flexible updating of stimulus–reward associations, with lateralization to the right hemisphere often implicated in aspects of emotional salience and behavioral inhibition.
