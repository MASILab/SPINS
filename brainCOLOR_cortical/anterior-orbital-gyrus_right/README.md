
# anterior-orbital-gyrus

## Overview

The right anterior orbital gyrus is a cortical region located on the ventral (orbital) surface of the right frontal lobe, overlying the orbits within the prefrontal cortex. It forms part of the orbitofrontal cortex (OFC), a multimodal association area involved in reward valuation, affective processing, decision-making, and the integration of sensory, emotional, and visceral information to guide behavior. Cytoarchitectonically, it corresponds to portions of the anterior orbital gyri described in classical frontal lobe maps and receives extensive input from limbic structures (including the amygdala), sensory association cortices, and subcortical nuclei, while projecting to other prefrontal and limbic regions. Functionally, activity in this region has been associated with encoding the subjective value of stimuli, updating reward contingencies, and regulating social and emotional behavior, and it shows altered structure or function in various neuropsychiatric conditions (e.g., depression, obsessive–compulsive disorder, and addiction).
