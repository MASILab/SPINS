
# precentral-gyrus

## Overview

The left precentral gyrus is a convolution of the frontal lobe located immediately anterior to the central sulcus and posterior to the superior, middle, and inferior frontal gyri, serving as the primary motor cortex (Brodmann area 4) for contralateral voluntary movement. In the brainCOLOR Atlas it is defined as the left-hemisphere counterpart of this motor region, typically bounded dorsally by the superior frontal gyrus and ventrally approaching the Sylvian (lateral) fissure, with its cytoarchitecture characterized by prominent Betz cells in layer V that project via the corticospinal and corticobulbar tracts. Functionally, it encodes somatotopically organized motor commands (motor homunculus), with medial portions controlling lower limbs and more lateral portions controlling face and upper limbs, integrating information from premotor and supplementary motor areas to execute skilled, precise movements. There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for “left precentral gyrus” or the brainCOLOR-defined region; a closely related and encompassing article is the general precentral gyrus entry: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precentral_gyrus.
