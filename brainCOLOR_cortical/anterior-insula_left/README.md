
# anterior-insula

## Overview

The left anterior insula is the rostral portion of the insular cortex located deep within the lateral sulcus of the left hemisphere, overlaid by the frontal and temporal opercula. Cytoarchitectonically, it is part of the agranular/dysgranular anterior insula, distinguished from the more granular posterior insula. It is heavily interconnected with limbic, frontal, and cingulo-opercular networks, including the anterior cingulate cortex, orbitofrontal cortex, amygdala, and prefrontal regions. Functionally, the left anterior insula is implicated in interoceptive awareness, integration of autonomic and visceral signals, affective processing, and higher-order cognitive control processes, and is a core node of the salience network that helps prioritize behaviorally relevant internal and external stimuli.
