
# central-operculum

## Overview

The right central operculum is a cortical region buried within the lateral sulcus, forming part of the opercular coverings of the insula and overlying the upper bank of the Sylvian fissure in the right hemisphere. In the brainCOLOR atlas, it is classified within the perisylvian opercular cortex and is closely associated with primary and secondary somatosensory areas and the ventral portions of the sensorimotor strip. Structurally, it lies between the frontal and parietal opercula and contributes to processing somatosensory information from the face, mouth, and orofacial musculature, as well as sensorimotor integration important for articulation and complex oral–facial movements. Functionally, activation in this region is often observed in tasks involving speech production, orofacial motor control, and multimodal sensory integration related to the perisylvian network. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “right central operculum”; a closely related structure is the operculum of the insula: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operculum_(frontal,_parietal,_and_temporal_lobes)
