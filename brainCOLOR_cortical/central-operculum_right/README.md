
# central-operculum

## Overview

The right central operculum is a cortical region forming part of the opercular cover overlying the insula in the right cerebral hemisphere, situated at the junction of the frontal, parietal, and temporal lobes along the lateral sulcus. It includes portions of peri-Sylvian cortex implicated in somatosensory and sensorimotor integration, particularly for orofacial and perisylvian functions, and participates in networks supporting speech articulation, phonological processing, and aspects of sensorimotor control. As part of the broader opercular system, it structurally covers the insular cortex and contributes to the interface between limbic/insular processing and neocortical sensorimotor functions. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “right central operculum” as defined in the brainCOLOR atlas; a closely related and encompassing structure is the operculum of the insula: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operculum_(brain)
