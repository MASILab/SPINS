
# superior-frontal-gyrus-medial-segment

## Overview

The left superior frontal gyrus, medial segment, is a dorsomedial frontal cortical region situated on the medial aspect of the superior frontal gyrus, extending anteriorly from the supplementary motor area (SMA) and pre-SMA toward the frontal pole. Cytoarchitectonically, it overlaps mainly with portions of Brodmann areas 8, 9, and medial 10, and is supplied predominantly by branches of the anterior cerebral artery. This region is implicated in higher-order executive functions, including working memory, prospective memory, and cognitive control, as well as internally directed thought, self-referential processing, and aspects of decision-making; it also participates in the default mode and frontoparietal control networks. Connectivity includes dense reciprocal projections with dorsolateral prefrontal cortex, anterior cingulate cortex, medial parietal regions (such as the precuneus), and subcortical structures including the striatum and thalamus, supporting its role in integrating motivational, cognitive, and motor-planning signals.
