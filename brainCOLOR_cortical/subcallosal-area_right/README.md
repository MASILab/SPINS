
# subcallosal-area

## Overview

The right subcallosal area is a ventromedial frontal lobe region located inferior to the genu of the corpus callosum and medial to the orbitofrontal cortex, corresponding roughly to Brodmann area 25 and often grouped within the subgenual cingulate or ventromedial prefrontal cortex. It is composed primarily of agranular to dysgranular cortex and associated white matter, receiving dense inputs from limbic structures such as the amygdala, hippocampal formation, and hypothalamus, as well as from medial prefrontal and anterior cingulate cortices. Efferent projections extend to limbic and autonomic centers including the hypothalamus, brainstem nuclei, and ventral striatum, supporting roles in affect regulation, visceromotor control, and autonomic responses to emotional stimuli. This region is heavily implicated in mood and anxiety disorders, particularly major depressive disorder, and has been a target for deep brain stimulation in treatment-resistant depression due to its involvement in negative affect, stress responses, and altered connectivity within fronto-limbic networks.
