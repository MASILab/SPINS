
# opercular-part-of-the-IFG

## Overview

The right opercular part of the inferior frontal gyrus (IFG) is a subdivision of the right frontal lobe located on the lateral surface, forming part of the ventrolateral prefrontal cortex and overlying the insula as part of the frontal operculum. It corresponds approximately to Brodmann area 44 on the right hemisphere and is situated posterior to the triangular part of the IFG and anterior to the precentral gyrus. Structurally, it is bordered superiorly by the inferior frontal sulcus and inferiorly by the lateral (Sylvian) fissure. Functionally, the right opercular IFG is implicated in motor aspects of speech and orofacial control, response inhibition, sensorimotor integration, and aspects of prosody and emotional intonation. It is part of a broader fronto-parietal network involved in cognitive control and may show lateralized contributions to nonverbal aspects of communication and inhibitory control.
