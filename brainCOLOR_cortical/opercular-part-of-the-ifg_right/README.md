
# opercular-part-of-the-IFG

## Overview

The right opercular part of the inferior frontal gyrus (IFGoperc R in the brainCOLOR atlas) is a subregion of the inferior frontal gyrus located in the posterior portion of the frontal lobe, overlying the anterior insula and bordering the precentral gyrus and the right frontal operculum. Cytoarchitectonically, it corresponds largely to Brodmann area 44 on the right hemisphere and is composed predominantly of granular frontal cortex with dense pyramidal cell layers that support corticocortical and corticosubcortical connectivity. Functionally, this region is implicated in speech and language processing (including aspects of phonological and syntactic operations), motor planning of orofacial movements, response inhibition, and broader cognitive control processes, often as a right-hemisphere counterpart to the language-dominant left IFG. Through extensive connections with temporal, parietal, premotor, and subcortical structures (such as the basal ganglia), the right opercular IFG contributes to sensorimotor integration, prosody, and the regulation of attention and executive functions.
