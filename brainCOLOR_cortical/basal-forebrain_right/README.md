
# Basal-Forebrain

## Overview

The right basal forebrain is a region located in the ventral aspect of the right cerebral hemisphere, anterior to the hypothalamus and below the anterior commissure and septal area, encompassing structures such as the nucleus basalis of Meynert, diagonal band nuclei, and parts of the medial septal region. It is a major source of cholinergic projections to the neocortex, amygdala, and hippocampus, playing a critical role in arousal, attention, learning, and memory. Degeneration of basal forebrain cholinergic neurons is strongly implicated in neurodegenerative disorders, particularly Alzheimer’s disease, where loss of cortical cholinergic input correlates with cognitive decline. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, this region is parcellated based on structural MRI landmarks and aligned with known cytoarchitectonic and connectivity features of the basal forebrain complex.
