
# triangular-part-of-the-IFG

## Overview

The right triangular part of the inferior frontal gyrus (right IFG pars triangularis) is a cortical subdivision of the inferior frontal gyrus located in the frontal lobe of the right hemisphere, interposed between the pars opercularis posteriorly and the pars orbitalis anteriorly. Cytoarchitectonically, it largely corresponds to Brodmann area 45, characterized by a granular frontal cortex with well-developed layer IV and prominent pyramidal neurons in layers III and V. This region participates in higher-order cognitive functions, including aspects of language processing (particularly semantic selection and controlled retrieval), response inhibition, and components of the ventral attention network, with strong structural and functional connectivity to the middle frontal gyrus, anterior insula, temporal cortex, and basal ganglia. It is supplied by branches of the middle cerebral artery and exhibits functional lateralization, with the right hemisphere homologue often implicated in non-dominant language functions, prosody, and inhibitory control.
