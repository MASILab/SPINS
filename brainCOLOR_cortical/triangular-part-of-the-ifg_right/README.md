
# triangular-part-of-the-IFG

## Overview

The right triangular part of the inferior frontal gyrus (IFG) is a cortical subdivision of the frontal lobe, corresponding cytoarchitectonically mainly to Brodmann area 45 (pars triangularis) in the right hemisphere. It is located on the lateral surface of the frontal lobe, bordered superiorly by the inferior frontal sulcus, inferiorly by the lateral fissure (Sylvian fissure), and lies between the opercular part (pars opercularis, BA44) posteriorly and the orbital part (pars orbitalis, BA47) anterior-inferiorly. This region is involved in higher-order cognitive functions including aspects of language processing (particularly in prosody and pragmatic aspects of speech in the right hemisphere), response inhibition, and controlled retrieval of semantic information, and it participates in large-scale networks such as the frontoparietal control network. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, it is parcellated as a distinct anatomical label within the broader inferior frontal gyrus, enabling standardized segmentation and analysis in structural and functional neuroimaging studies.
