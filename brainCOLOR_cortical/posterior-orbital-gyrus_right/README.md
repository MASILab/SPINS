
# posterior-orbital-gyrus

## Overview

The right posterior orbital gyrus is a cortical region located on the ventral surface of the frontal lobe, within the orbitofrontal cortex, just above the orbits and toward the posterior (caudal) part of the orbital gyri. It is involved in higher-order association processes linked to reward evaluation, decision-making, emotional regulation, and the integration of sensory and limbic information that supports adaptive behavior. As part of the orbitofrontal cortex, it contributes to assigning value to stimuli, updating outcome expectations, and modifying behavior based on changing environmental contingencies.
