## Overview

The left lateral orbital gyrus is a cortical region on the ventrolateral surface of the frontal lobe, forming part of the orbitofrontal cortex situated above the orbits. In the brainCOLOR atlas, it is designated as a distinct parcellation within the orbital gyri, which are involved in higher-order associative and integrative functions. Functionally, the lateral orbital gyrus participates in reward valuation, decision-making, emotional regulation, and the integration of sensory and visceral information to guide goal-directed behavior and social cognition. It has dense connections with limbic structures (e.g., amygdala), medial prefrontal areas, and sensory association cortices, supporting its role in affective processing and adaptive behavioral control.
