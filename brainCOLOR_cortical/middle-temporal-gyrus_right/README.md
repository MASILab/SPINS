
# middle-temporal-gyrus

## Overview

The right middle temporal gyrus (right MTG) is a longitudinal cortical region located on the lateral surface of the right temporal lobe, positioned between the superior and inferior temporal gyri and extending from the temporal pole anteriorly toward the temporo-parietal junction posteriorly. Cytoarchitectonically, it includes portions of Brodmann areas 21 and 37, with laminar organization typical of association cortex, characterized by well-developed supragranular layers that support cortico-cortical connectivity. The right MTG participates in multimodal integration, including aspects of semantic processing, social cognition, visual perception of objects and motion, and higher-order auditory processing, and it maintains extensive reciprocal connections with other temporal, frontal, and parietal association areas, as well as limbic structures. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, “Right middle-temporal-gyrus” refers specifically to this anatomically defined gyrus on the right hemisphere, segmented according to sulcal boundaries and hemispheric laterality conventions used in structural neuroimaging.
