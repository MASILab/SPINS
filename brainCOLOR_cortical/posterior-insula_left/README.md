
# posterior-insula

## Overview

The left posterior insula is a cortical region located within the posterior portion of the insular lobe, buried deep in the lateral sulcus and covered by the frontal, parietal, and temporal opercula. It is primarily associated with interoceptive processing, including visceral sensation, thermosensation, pain perception, and aspects of vestibular and somatosensory integration. Cytoarchitectonically, the posterior insula is more granular than the anterior insula and shows strong reciprocal connections with primary and secondary somatosensory cortices, posterior parietal regions, thalamic nuclei, and brainstem autonomic centers, supporting roles in sensorimotor integration and autonomic regulation. Functional imaging studies implicate the left posterior insula in lateralized processing of bodily states, modulation of nociceptive input, and integration of sensory information into conscious bodily awareness.
