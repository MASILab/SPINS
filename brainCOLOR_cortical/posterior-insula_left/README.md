
# posterior-insula

## Overview

The left posterior insula is a cortical subdivision of the insular lobe situated deep within the lateral sulcus, posterior to the central insular sulcus, and covered by the opercula of the frontal, parietal, and temporal lobes. It is cytoarchitectonically distinct from the anterior insula, with a more granular laminar organization, and is strongly interconnected with primary and secondary somatosensory cortices, posterior parietal regions, thalamic nuclei, and brainstem autonomic centers. Functionally, the left posterior insula is implicated in interoceptive and somatosensory processing, including nociception, temperature, visceral sensations, and aspects of sensorimotor integration, and contributes to autonomic regulation and the mapping of bodily states. In the brainCOLOR atlas, it is parcellated as a specific ROI within the insular cortex based on anatomical boundaries and structural MRI features, serving as a node within distributed networks for bodily awareness and homeostatic control. There is no direct Wikipedia article for the “left posterior insula” as a separate entry; a closely related structure is the insular cortex: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insular_cortex
