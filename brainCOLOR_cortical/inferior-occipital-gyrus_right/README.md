
# inferior-occipital-gyrus

## Overview

The right inferior occipital gyrus is a convoluted cortical region on the ventrolateral surface of the occipital lobe, forming part of the visual association cortex (predominantly Brodmann area 18/19). It is located inferior to the lateral occipital cortex and often bordered ventrally by the occipitotemporal (fusiform) gyrus, participating in early to intermediate stages of visual processing. Neurons in this region contribute to analysis of basic and mid‑level visual attributes, including shape, contour, and aspects of object features that are subsequently integrated in more anterior ventral visual stream areas involved in object, face, and word recognition. Functionally, it maintains strong reciprocal connections with primary visual cortex (V1) and extrastriate areas, as well as with temporal visual regions, supporting hierarchical processing of visual information. There is no direct Wikipedia page dedicated specifically to the “right inferior occipital gyrus”; a closely related and encompassing structure is described at: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occipital_lobe
