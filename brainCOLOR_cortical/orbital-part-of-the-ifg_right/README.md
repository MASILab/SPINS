
# orbital-part-of-the-IFG

## Overview

The right orbital part of the inferior frontal gyrus (right orbital-part-of-the-IFG) is a ventral subdivision of the inferior frontal gyrus located on the orbital surface of the frontal lobe, overlying the roof of the orbit within the prefrontal cortex. It corresponds to portions of Brodmann area 47 (and sometimes parts of 11/12, depending on parcellation) and is involved in higher-order cognitive and affective processes, including aspects of semantic processing, reward evaluation, decision-making, and social and emotional cognition. This region is structurally interconnected with other prefrontal, limbic, and temporal areas, including the orbitofrontal cortex, anterior cingulate cortex, and temporal pole, supporting its role in integrating sensory, emotional, and motivational information to guide behavior. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “right orbital-part-of-the-IFG” as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas; a closely related structure is the orbitofrontal cortex: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orbitofrontal_cortex.
