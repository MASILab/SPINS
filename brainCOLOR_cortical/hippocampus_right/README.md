
# Hippocampus

## Overview

The right hippocampus is the right-sided component of the hippocampal formation, a bilateral medial temporal lobe structure critical for episodic memory, spatial navigation, and contextual processing. It is an elongated, curved gray matter structure situated within the floor of the temporal horn of the lateral ventricle and is composed of cornu ammonis (CA1–CA4), dentate gyrus, and subiculum, with extensive reciprocal connections to the entorhinal cortex, amygdala, and distributed neocortical association areas. Functionally, the right hippocampus is particularly implicated in visuospatial memory, scene construction, and navigation, and is highly plastic but vulnerable to injury, neurodegeneration, and epileptogenic processes. It participates in hippocampal–cortical networks supporting memory consolidation and retrieval and exhibits longitudinal and transverse intrinsic circuitry that enables pattern separation and completion.
