
# Pallidum

## Overview

The left pallidum, as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas, corresponds to the left globus pallidus, a major component of the basal ganglia situated medial to the putamen and lateral to the internal capsule within the telencephalon. It is typically divided into an external (lateral) segment and an internal (medial) segment, both composed predominantly of GABAergic neurons that exert inhibitory control over thalamic and brainstem motor nuclei. Through its extensive connectivity with the striatum, subthalamic nucleus, and thalamus, the left pallidum participates critically in the regulation of voluntary movement, muscle tone, and procedural learning, integrating cortical input and shaping the output of basal ganglia–thalamo–cortical loops. Dysfunction of this region is implicated in movement disorders such as Parkinson’s disease, dystonia, and chorea, in which abnormal pallidal activity contributes to impaired initiation, scaling, and coordination of movements.
