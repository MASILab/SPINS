
# precentral-gyrus-medial-segment

## Overview

The right precentral gyrus medial segment refers to the medial portion of the primary motor cortex (Brodmann area 4) located on the right hemisphere along the anterior bank of the central sulcus, extending toward the interhemispheric fissure. This region is somatotopically organized, with a predominance of motor representations for the contralateral lower limb and axial musculature, including movements of the leg, foot, and trunk, as part of the “motor homunculus.” It contains large pyramidal neurons (Betz cells) whose axons contribute to the corticospinal and corticobulbar tracts, thus playing a critical role in voluntary motor control, particularly in the execution rather than planning of movements. The medial segment also interacts with supplementary motor and premotor regions to modulate posture, gait, and coordinated bilateral actions, and it integrates sensory feedback to refine motor output.
