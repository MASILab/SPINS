
# precentral-gyrus-medial-segment

## Overview

The right precentral gyrus medial segment is the medial portion of the precentral gyrus located on the lateral aspect of the right frontal lobe, immediately anterior to the central sulcus and extending medially toward the interhemispheric fissure. It forms part of the primary motor cortex (Brodmann area 4) and supplementary motor-related regions, containing a somatotopic representation of contralateral body parts, especially those closer to the midline such as the lower limb and axial musculature. Neurons in this region project via the corticospinal and corticobulbar tracts to spinal and brainstem motor nuclei, contributing to the initiation, execution, and fine control of voluntary movement, as well as coordination of complex motor sequences. It participates in motor planning in conjunction with premotor and supplementary motor areas and is integrated with sensorimotor, cerebellar, and basal ganglia circuits for the regulation of muscle tone, posture, and movement precision.
