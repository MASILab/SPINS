
# Cerebellar-Vermal-Lobules-VIII-X

## Overview

The Midline Cerebellar-Vermal-Lobules-VIII–X region corresponds to the midline (vermal) portions of the posterior inferior cerebellar vermis, encompassing vermal lobules VIII, IX, and X (nodulus). These lobules form part of the spinocerebellum and vestibulocerebellum and are involved in postural control, balance, ocular movements, and coordination of axial and proximal limb musculature. Vermal lobule VIII participates in sensorimotor integration for trunk and gait control, lobule IX contributes to vestibular and visuomotor functions and integration of multimodal sensory input for balance, and lobule X (the nodulus) is a key component of the vestibulocerebellum, heavily interconnected with vestibular nuclei and critical for processing head motion, vestibulo-ocular reflexes, and spatial orientation. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, this region is segmented as a midline structure along the dorsal surface of the posterior fossa, bounded laterally by the cerebellar hemispheric lobules VIII–X and connected inferiorly with flocculonodular structures and brainstem vestibular pathways.
