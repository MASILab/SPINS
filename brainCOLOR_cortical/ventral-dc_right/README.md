## Overview

The Right Ventral-DC region in the brainCOLOR atlas refers to the right ventral portion of the diencephalon (DC), a major subdivision of the forebrain that includes structures such as the thalamus and hypothalamus. In anatomical and functional terms, the ventral diencephalic areas participate in relaying and modulating sensory, motor, autonomic, and endocrine information between the brainstem, basal forebrain, and cerebral cortex. These regions contain nuclei involved in arousal, homeostatic regulation (including temperature, hunger, and circadian rhythms), and neuroendocrine control via connections with the pituitary gland, as well as pathways important for integrating visceral and somatic signals.
