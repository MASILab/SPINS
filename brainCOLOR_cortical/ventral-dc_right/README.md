
# Ventral-DC

## Overview

The Right Ventral-DC region in the brainCOLOR atlas refers to the right ventral portion of the diencephalon (DC), a major forebrain division situated between the telencephalon and the midbrain. The ventral diencephalon includes hypothalamic and subthalamic territories and related ventral thalamic structures that are involved in autonomic regulation, endocrine control, motivated behavior, and modulation of motor and limbic circuits. As part of the right hemisphere, the Right Ventral-DC encompasses nuclei and fiber pathways that integrate visceral, neuroendocrine, and behavioral responses, receiving inputs from cortical, brainstem, and limbic areas and projecting widely to the cortex, brainstem, and spinal cord.
