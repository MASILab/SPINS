
# middle-occipital-gyrus

## Overview

The left middle occipital gyrus is a cortical region located on the lateral surface of the occipital lobe, situated between the superior and inferior occipital gyri and extending anteriorly toward the occipito-temporal junction. It is predominantly composed of visual association cortex and participates in higher-order visual processing, including analysis of motion, form, and spatial aspects of visual stimuli, as well as contributing to visuospatial attention and object recognition. Functionally, it forms part of large-scale visual networks connecting with dorsal and ventral visual stream regions, receiving input from primary visual cortex (V1) and projecting to parietal and temporal association areas. Cytoarchitectonically, it encompasses portions of Brodmann areas within the occipital lobe beyond primary visual cortex, reflecting a role in integrative rather than primary sensory processing.
