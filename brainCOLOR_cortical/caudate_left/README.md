
# Caudate

## Overview

The left caudate nucleus is the left-sided component of the caudate, a paired C-shaped gray matter structure within the dorsal striatum of the basal ganglia, situated medial to the internal capsule and lateral ventricle and extending from the head in the frontal lobe through the body to the tail in the temporal lobe. It receives dense glutamatergic inputs from widespread cortical areas and dopaminergic projections from the substantia nigra pars compacta, and it sends GABAergic outputs primarily to the globus pallidus and substantia nigra pars reticulata, forming part of the cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical loops involved in motor control, procedural learning, motivation, and aspects of cognition and executive function. While the brainCOLOR atlas labels this region specifically as “Left Caudate,” most anatomical and functional descriptions are not lateralized; there is no dedicated Wikipedia page for “Left Caudate,” but the closely related and encompassing structure is described here: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caudate_nucleus.
