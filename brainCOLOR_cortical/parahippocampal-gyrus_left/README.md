
# parahippocampal-gyrus

## Overview

The left parahippocampal gyrus is a medial temporal lobe structure that lies along the ventromedial surface of the cerebral hemisphere, bordering the hippocampus and extending from the temporal pole toward the isthmus of the cingulate gyrus. Cytoarchitectonically, it includes areas such as the entorhinal cortex and parahippocampal cortex, which serve as major interfaces between neocortex and the hippocampal formation. Functionally, the left parahippocampal gyrus is strongly implicated in episodic memory, contextual and spatial processing, and scene recognition, with the left hemisphere often more associated with verbal and narrative memory encoding. It participates in networks supporting navigation, retrieval of contextual details, and associative memory, and shows robust activation in imaging studies during tasks involving recognition of places, environmental layouts, and context-rich stimuli. Clinically, pathology affecting this region is linked to memory impairment, temporal lobe epilepsy, and certain neurodegenerative conditions.
