
# occipital-fusiform-gyrus

## Overview

The right occipital-fusiform gyrus is a ventral temporo-occipital cortical region situated along the medial-inferior surface of the right hemisphere, spanning the junction between the occipital lobe and the fusiform gyrus of the temporal lobe. It participates in high-level visual processing, including object and complex pattern recognition, and contributes to category-selective visual functions such as face and word form processing as part of the ventral visual (“what”) pathway. Cytoarchitectonically, it overlaps with regions that contain the fusiform face area (FFA) and portions of the visual word form area (VWFA), integrating inputs from early visual cortices and projecting to anterior temporal and frontal regions involved in semantic and associative processing. This region is richly interconnected through inferior longitudinal and inferior fronto-occipital fasciculi, supporting rapid transmission of detailed visual information to downstream cognitive and memory systems. There is no direct Wikipedia page for “Right occipital-fusiform gyrus”; a closely related structure is the fusiform gyrus: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fusiform_gyrus
