
# Pallidum

## Overview

The right pallidum is the right-hemisphere component of the globus pallidus, a major basal ganglia structure located medial to the putamen and lateral to the internal capsule, and is typically subdivided into external (GPe) and internal (GPi) segments. Composed primarily of GABAergic projection neurons, it plays a central role in the regulation of voluntary movement, motor learning, and action selection by integrating inhibitory and excitatory inputs from the striatum and subthalamic nucleus and relaying processed signals to thalamic nuclei and brainstem motor centers. Through its participation in the indirect and direct pathways of the basal ganglia circuitry, the right pallidum contributes to the facilitation of intended movements and the suppression of competing or unwanted motor programs, with dysfunction implicated in movement disorders such as Parkinson’s disease, dystonia, and chorea.
