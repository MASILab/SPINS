
# superior-temporal-gyrus

## Overview

The right superior temporal gyrus (R STG), as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas, is a cortical region located on the lateral aspect of the right temporal lobe, bounded superiorly by the lateral (Sylvian) fissure and inferiorly by the superior temporal sulcus. It extends from the temporal pole anteriorly toward the temporoparietal junction posteriorly, and includes primary and secondary auditory cortices as well as association areas involved in higher-order auditory processing, language-related functions (particularly aspects of speech perception and prosody), and social cognition. Cytoarchitectonically, it comprises several Brodmann areas (notably 22 and portions of 41/42 in its posterior-medial extent), and it is interconnected with frontal, parietal, limbic, and other temporal regions through major white-matter pathways such as the arcuate fasciculus and superior longitudinal fasciculus. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, the R STG is represented as a laterally placed temporal cortical label used for structural parcellation and volumetric or surface-based analyses, facilitating region-specific morphometric and connectivity studies.

There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for the “Right superior temporal gyrus” as a separate entry; a closely related structure is the (bilateral) superior temporal gyrus: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superior_temporal_gyrus
