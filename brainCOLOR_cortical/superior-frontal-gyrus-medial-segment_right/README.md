
# superior-frontal-gyrus-medial-segment

## Overview

The right superior frontal gyrus, medial segment, is a dorsomedial prefrontal cortical region located on the medial aspect of the superior frontal gyrus in the right hemisphere, extending anterior to the supplementary motor area and bordering the medial prefrontal and cingulate cortices. It is composed predominantly of granular and dysgranular prefrontal cortex and participates in high-level executive functions, including working memory, attentional control, decision-making, and aspects of self-referential and social cognition; it also interfaces with motor planning networks via connections with premotor and supplementary motor regions, and with limbic circuitry through reciprocal projections to the anterior cingulate and orbitomedial prefrontal areas. In functional neuroimaging, this region is frequently implicated in tasks requiring cognitive control, response inhibition, and internal mentation, and is commonly considered part of the broader medial prefrontal network within the default mode and executive control systems.
