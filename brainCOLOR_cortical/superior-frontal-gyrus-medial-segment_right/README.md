
# superior-frontal-gyrus-medial-segment

## Overview

The right superior frontal gyrus, medial segment, is a medial prefrontal cortical region located on the dorsomedial surface of the frontal lobe, anterior to the precentral gyrus and superior to the cingulate sulcus. It corresponds largely to portions of the medial superior frontal cortex that encompass parts of Brodmann areas 8, 9, and/or 10, and participates in higher-order executive functions, self-referential processing, decision-making, and aspects of working memory and attention. This region is interconnected with other prefrontal areas, anterior cingulate cortex, parietal association cortices, and subcortical structures, contributing to cognitive control, internally directed thought, and modulation of goal-directed behavior.
