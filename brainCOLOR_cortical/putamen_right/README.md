
# Putamen

## Overview

The right putamen is the right-sided component of the putamen, a large, rounded, subcortical gray matter structure forming the lateral portion of the dorsal striatum within the basal ganglia. It lies lateral to the internal capsule and medial to the external capsule and insular cortex, and is continuous ventromedially with the head of the caudate nucleus via the nucleus accumbens. Composed predominantly of medium spiny GABAergic projection neurons, the right putamen receives dense glutamatergic input from sensorimotor and associative cortices, as well as dopaminergic input from the substantia nigra pars compacta, and sends inhibitory outputs via the globus pallidus and substantia nigra pars reticulata to thalamic and brainstem targets. Functionally, it participates in motor planning, execution, and habit learning, as well as aspects of reward-based and procedural learning, and is implicated in movement disorders (e.g., Parkinson’s disease, dystonia), neuropsychiatric conditions, and responses to psychotropic drugs.
