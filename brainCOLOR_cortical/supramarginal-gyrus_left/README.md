
# supramarginal-gyrus

## Overview

The left supramarginal gyrus is a cortical region of the inferior parietal lobule, arching around the posterior end of the lateral (Sylvian) fissure, and is cytoarchitectonically associated mainly with Brodmann area 40. It is implicated in phonological processing, verbal working memory, and aspects of reading and language comprehension, as well as in sensorimotor integration and spatial attention. The region forms part of a dorsal language network, interacting with frontal language areas via the arcuate fasciculus, and shows left-hemispheric dominance in most right-handed individuals. Functionally, it contributes to the mapping of auditory and visual linguistic inputs onto articulatory codes, and is also involved in higher-order somatosensory functions such as the perception of body schema and tool use.
