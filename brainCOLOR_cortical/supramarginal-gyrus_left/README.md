
# supramarginal-gyrus

## Overview

The left supramarginal gyrus is a cortical region of the inferior parietal lobule, wrapping around the posterior end of the lateral (Sylvian) fissure and bordering the angular gyrus posteriorly. It is cytoarchitectonically associated largely with Brodmann area 40 and is strongly interconnected with perisylvian language regions, premotor areas, and somatosensory cortices. Functionally, the left supramarginal gyrus contributes to phonological processing, verbal working memory, grapheme–phoneme conversion, and aspects of speech production, as well as sensorimotor integration for orofacial and limb movements. It is also implicated in higher-order functions such as reading, spelling, calculation, and aspects of social cognition, and is a frequent node in language-dominant (usually left) hemispheric networks.
