
# occipital-pole

## Overview

The right occipital pole is the most posterior portion of the right occipital lobe, corresponding to the tip of the occipital cortex and encompassing parts of the primary and adjacent visual areas. It lies posterior to the parieto-occipital and calcarine sulci, with cortical tissue surrounding the termination of the calcarine fissure that contains primary visual cortex (V1, Brodmann area 17) and nearby secondary visual regions. This region is critical for initial processing of visual information such as orientation, spatial frequency, and basic contrast patterns, contributing to subsequent higher-order visual functions in dorsal and ventral visual streams. The right-sided localization can influence lateralized visual processing, including aspects of visuospatial analysis and representation of the left visual hemifield. There is no direct Wikipedia page for the “Right occipital pole” as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas; a closely related structure is the primary visual cortex: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primary_visual_cortex
