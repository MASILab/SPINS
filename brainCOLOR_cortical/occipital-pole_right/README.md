
# occipital-pole

## Overview

The right occipital pole is the most posterior portion of the right occipital lobe and represents a key cortical region for early visual processing, corresponding largely to posterior segments of primary visual cortex (V1) and adjacent early visual areas. It receives highly organized retinotopic input from the lateral geniculate nucleus of the thalamus and is involved in processing basic visual features such as luminance contrast, orientation, spatial frequency, and simple motion, particularly for stimuli in the central visual field. Neural activity in this region supports the initial stages of transforming retinal input into structured visual representations that are subsequently relayed to dorsal and ventral visual streams for spatial analysis and object recognition. The right occipital pole also contributes to interhemispheric integration of visual information via callosal connections with the corresponding left occipital pole and exhibits strong structural and functional connectivity with extrastriate visual cortices.
