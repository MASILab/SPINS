
# occipital-pole

## Overview

The left occipital pole is the most posterior portion of the left occipital lobe, forming the caudal tip of the cerebral hemisphere and encompassing parts of the primary visual cortex (V1, Brodmann area 17) and adjacent visual association areas. It lies posterior to the parieto-occipital and calcarine sulci and is principally supplied by branches of the posterior cerebral artery. Cytoarchitectonically, the region is characterized by a prominent line of Gennari in layer IV of the cortex, reflecting dense geniculostriate input from the lateral geniculate nucleus of the thalamus. Functionally, the left occipital pole participates in early-stage processing of visual information, particularly high-resolution foveal input, and provides feedforward projections to higher-order visual areas in the occipital and temporal lobes that support object recognition, reading, and visually guided behaviors.
