
# frontal-operculum

## Overview

The right frontal operculum is a cortical region located on the ventrolateral surface of the right frontal lobe, overlying portions of the insula and bordering the lateral (Sylvian) fissure. It corresponds largely to the opercular parts of the inferior frontal gyrus (including portions of Brodmann areas 44, 45, and adjacent 47), and forms part of the frontal opercular complex that covers the anterior insula. Functionally, the right frontal operculum has been implicated in language prosody, response inhibition, cognitive control, and aspects of sensorimotor integration, with strong structural and functional connectivity to the insula, premotor regions, and inferior parietal cortices. In the brainCOLOR Atlas, it is typically parcellated as a distinct label reflecting this opercular portion of the right inferior frontal cortex. There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for the “right frontal operculum” as defined in the brainCOLOR Atlas; a closely related and encompassing structure is the frontal operculum: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frontal_operculum.
