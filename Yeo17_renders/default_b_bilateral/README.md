
# Default_B

## Overview

The Bilateral Default_B region in the Yeo-17 functional atlas is a subdivision of the default mode network (DMN), a large-scale intrinsic connectivity network associated with internally directed cognition. Default_B typically includes midline and lateral regions such as portions of the medial prefrontal cortex, posterior cingulate/precuneus, and angular or inferior parietal areas, though exact boundaries vary by parcellation. Functionally, this subdivision is implicated in self-referential processing, autobiographical memory, mentalizing (theory of mind), and the integration of internally generated information. It exhibits high activity during rest and reduced activation during externally focused, attention-demanding tasks, reflecting its role in introspective and associative processes.
