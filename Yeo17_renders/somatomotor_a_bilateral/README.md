
# Somatomotor_A

## Overview

The Bilateral Somatomotor_A region in the Yeo-17 functional atlas corresponds primarily to core areas of the primary somatomotor network, encompassing regions of the precentral gyrus (primary motor cortex, M1) and adjacent postcentral gyrus (primary somatosensory cortex, S1) in both hemispheres. This network supports the initiation, execution, and modulation of voluntary movements and the processing of somatosensory feedback from the body, including proprioceptive, tactile, and to some extent nociceptive inputs. Neurons in these regions are somatotopically organized, forming a motor and sensory homunculus in which different body parts are represented along the dorsal–ventral axis of the central sulcus. Functionally, the Bilateral Somatomotor_A network shows strong intrinsic connectivity at rest and is consistently engaged during tasks involving limb and facial movements, motor planning, sensorimotor integration, and fine motor control.
