## Overview

The Bilateral Dorsal_Attention_B network in the Yeo-17 atlas is a subdivision of the dorsal attention system, comprising regions in the intraparietal sulcus and superior parietal lobule, together with dorsal frontal areas such as the frontal eye fields, represented in both hemispheres. This network is implicated in top-down, goal-directed allocation of visual attention, spatial orienting, and the voluntary selection of sensory information, often in contrast to the ventral attention network, which is more stimulus-driven. Functional neuroimaging studies associate this network with tasks requiring sustained attention, visuospatial processing, and eye-movement control, indicating a central role in coordinating perception and action.
