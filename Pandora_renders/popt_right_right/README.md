
# Parieto-occipital pontine right

## Overview

The right parieto-occipital pontine tract (right parieto-occipital pontine functional group) in the Pandora-TractSeg Atlas is a long association/projection fiber pathway that links cortical regions of the right parietal and occipital lobes with pontine nuclei in the brainstem. Originating primarily near the parieto-occipital junction, this tract courses inferomedially through the deep white matter and internal capsule toward the ventral pons, where pontine neurons relay signals to the cerebellum via pontocerebellar fibers. Functionally, this pathway participates in visuospatial integration, visuomotor coordination, and higher-order sensorimotor transformations by channeling processed visual and parietal information to pontine and cerebellar circuits that refine motor planning, eye–hand coordination, and balance of visual–proprioceptive inputs.
