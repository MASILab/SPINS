
# Corticospinal tract right

## Overview

The right corticospinal tract (CST) is a major descending white matter pathway originating primarily from pyramidal neurons in layer V of the right primary motor cortex, premotor cortex, and supplementary motor areas, with contributions from somatosensory regions. Axons from these neurons descend through the corona radiata and the posterior limb of the internal capsule, continue through the cerebral peduncle of the midbrain, the ventral pons, and the medullary pyramids, where the majority decussate at the pyramidal decussation to form the left lateral corticospinal tract in the spinal cord; a smaller proportion remains uncrossed as the anterior corticospinal tract. Functionally, the right CST is crucial for voluntary, fine, and fractionated movements of the contralateral (left) side of the body, especially distal musculature of the limbs, by providing direct and indirect monosynaptic and polysynaptic input to spinal motor neurons and interneurons. There is no direct Wikipedia page specifically for the “right corticospinal tract” as defined in the Pandora-TractSeg Atlas; a closely related and encompassing entry is the general corticospinal tract page: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corticospinal_tract
