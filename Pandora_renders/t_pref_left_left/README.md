
# Thalamo-prefrontal left

## Overview

The left thalamo-prefrontal tract (Pandora-TractSeg Atlas) is a major associative white matter pathway linking the mediodorsal and other associative nuclei of the left thalamus with prefrontal cortical regions, including dorsolateral, ventrolateral, and medial prefrontal areas. This tract supports higher-order cognitive functions such as working memory, executive control, decision-making, and aspects of emotional regulation by integrating thalamic relay and modulatory signals with prefrontal processing networks. Structurally, it traverses the anterior limb of the internal capsule and adjacent periventricular white matter, forming part of fronto-thalamic circuits that are critical for goal-directed behavior and conscious cognitive operations. Dysfunction or disruption of this pathway has been implicated in neuropsychiatric and neurocognitive disorders, including schizophrenia, mood disorders, and frontal lobe syndromes.
