
# Striato-postcentral right

## Overview

The right Striato-postcentral region in the Pandora-TractSeg atlas refers to a projection-defined functional tract system linking portions of the striatum (a major subcortical component of the basal ganglia) with the postcentral gyrus in the right hemisphere, which is the primary somatosensory cortex. This tract group likely carries processed sensorimotor and somatosensory information involved in the integration of bodily sensation with action selection, habit learning, and motor modulation, reflecting the broader role of corticostriatal loops in coordinating perception, movement, and reward-based learning. Structurally, it encompasses white-matter fibers that originate in or pass through the right striatum (including caudate and putamen) and terminate in or near Brodmann areas 1–3 of the postcentral gyrus, forming part of the sensorimotor corticostriatal circuitry.
