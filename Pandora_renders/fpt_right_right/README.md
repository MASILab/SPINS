
# Fronto-pontine tract right

## Overview

The right fronto-pontine tract is a longitudinal white matter pathway connecting frontal cortical regions of the right hemisphere—particularly prefrontal and premotor areas—with nuclei in the pons, forming part of the fronto-pontine component of the cortico-ponto-cerebellar system. It courses through the anterior limb of the internal capsule and the cerebral peduncle, carrying primarily efferent fibers that convey higher-order motor planning, cognitive, and executive signals from the frontal lobe to pontine relay neurons, which then project to the cerebellum via pontocerebellar fibers. Functionally, this tract participates in the integration of executive control, motor coordination, and possibly aspects of attention and working memory, by enabling communication between frontal cortical areas and cerebellar networks involved in fine-tuning motor and cognitive operations.
