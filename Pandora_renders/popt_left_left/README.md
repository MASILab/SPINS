
# Parieto-occipital pontine left

## Overview

The left parieto-occipital pontine tract, as defined in the Pandora-TractSeg atlas, is a long association and projection fiber pathway that connects cortical regions at the parieto-occipital junction of the left hemisphere with pontine nuclei in the brainstem. Originating mainly from posterior parietal and occipital cortical areas involved in visuospatial integration, motion processing, and higher-order visual perception, these fibers descend through the deep white matter and internal capsule region toward the pons, where they contribute to cortico-pontine projections that relay information to the cerebellum via pontocerebellar pathways. Functionally, this tract participates in circuits that integrate visual, spatial, and sensorimotor information for coordination of eye movements, posture, and fine-tuned motor control. There is no direct Wikipedia entry for the “left parieto-occipital pontine” tract; a closely related structure and target region is the pons: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pons
